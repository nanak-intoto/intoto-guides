Platform & access

What is Intoto?
A white-label campus engagement platform that brings a university's people together in one place. Incoming students onboard, complete travel and arrival details, discover their campus, join communities, and register for events — while staff and partner organisations run the experience through role-specific tools.
What paid features does Intoto sell to universities?
Seven standalone modules: Ambassador Management, Travel Plan, Community, Chat, Event Management, User Management, and Mobility Program. Universities can buy one, several, or all — and add more at any time. See the Paid Features catalog for full detail.
Can a university subscribe to only some features?
Yes. Intoto is à la carte, not all-or-nothing. A common starting point is User Management plus Chat; modules like Travel Plan, Event Management, or Mobility Program are added when the institution is ready.
Who provisions features — Intoto Admin or the university?
Intoto Admin provisions modules during onboarding or on request from sales. The University Super Admin sees Active status and contract dates on My University → Features but cannot toggle modules themselves.
How many roles are there?
Ten in-app roles plus web product guides and the Paid Features catalog: Intoto Admin, Student, Ambassador, External Ambassador, Community Coordinator, Event Coordinator, Mobility Program Admin, University Super Admin, Third Party Coordinator, Third Party Sub Coordinator, The Ambassador Platform (public web), and Paid Features (product catalog). Each role guide is linked above.
What is the Intoto Admin, and how is it different from a University Super Admin?
The Intoto Admin is the platform-level administrator — they operate across every university on Intoto, onboard and manage the universities (tenants) themselves, invite top-level University Super Admins, and can switch university context to drill into one tenant. A University Super Admin runs a single university on the web portal and mobile app — user lifecycle, profile review, configuration, and read-only oversight of other roles. Only the Intoto Admin can add, suspend, or activate a university.
Can one person hold more than one role?
Yes. Multi-role accounts pick a role at Role Selection on login, or switch later from the role switcher. The home and tabs reload for whichever role is active, keeping each context cleanly separate.
What does “scoped to one university and campus” mean?
Every role assignment is tied to a single university (and a campus where configured). Working across more than one university is handled as separate role assignments you switch between — not a single cross-university view.
How do invitations work?
Invited users see an alert at login with Accept or I'll do it later (deferred invites wait under the envelope icon). Some roles confirm a campus or type on accept — for example External Ambassadors confirm the External ambassador type.
Why does my dashboard look different from someone else's?
Home is backend-driven: the server sends only the sections relevant to your role, in the order it defines. A coordinator sees governance and queues; an ambassador sees outreach cards; a student sees onboarding and discovery.
What are the four tabs?
Home, Community, Chats, and Profile are always present. What each surfaces is tuned to the active role, but the structure stays consistent across the app.
How is student data kept safe?
Sign-in is via Auth0, on-device storage is encrypted, and uploaded documents are checked for tampering. Visibility rules further limit what each role can see — for example, what a sub-coordinator sees about an assigned student.

Students & ambassadors

How does a student get started?
A student signs in, completes onboarding and their travel/arrival details, and lands on a Home dashboard built for discovery — finding their campus, communities, events, and ambassadors. Forms save as they go so progress isn't lost.
Why does profile completion matter so much?
Completion drives what students unlock and how ambassadors appear to them. For ambassadors specifically, an incomplete profile is hidden or ranked lower on the student Ambassadors carousel, so completeness directly affects visibility.
How do students find an ambassador?
Two paths: enrolled students use the Ambassadors carousel on the mobile Home dashboard; prospective students use The Ambassador Platform web directory — search, filter, open a card, tap Chat. External ambassadors are especially visible on the platform under the Staff filter.
What's the difference between an Ambassador and an External Ambassador?
An Ambassador is usually a current student or alumnus on the mobile app (often also holding the Student role). An External Ambassador is an off-campus partner — recruitment agency, regional rep, or alumnus abroad — who works from the web portal, appears on The Ambassador Platform with a Staff badge, and manages student leads plus chat from the browser.
What is the Ambassador Info section?
It's the public, mentor-facing part of the profile: bio, ambassador type, programs supported, languages, availability, and interests. Everything a student sees on a mentor card comes from here.
Can an ambassador message many students at once?
No. Ambassador messaging is one-to-one and support-oriented. Mass communication to the student body is a staff/coordinator capability, not an ambassador one.
Can an ambassador approve events or communities?
No. Ambassadors participate — register for events, join and post in communities — but approving events belongs to the Event Coordinator and approving communities to the Community Coordinator or University Super Admin.
What does an ambassador do day to day?
Two things: keep a complete, current mentor profile so students can find them, and answer the students who reach out in Chats — questions about arrival, housing, registration, events, and campus life.

University staff

What does the Community Coordinator do?
They review, publish, and moderate communities — clearing the pending queue, approving or rejecting new groups, and keeping student communities safe and on-policy within their university.
How does community approval work?
New communities land in a pending queue. The coordinator reviews each one and approves it to publish or rejects it with a reason. Students only ever see approved communities.
What does the Event Coordinator do?
They run campus events end to end — creating and editing events, clearing the pending event queue, tracking registrations and attendance, and reading events-by-category analytics to plan programming.
How does an event get published?
A coordinator creates the event with schedule, venue, registration window, image, and category, then saves or submits for approval. Depending on tenant rules it lands in Pending or publishes to Upcoming; approval is the gate that puts it in front of students.
What do Upcoming, Pending, and Completed mean for events?
Upcoming are published future events students can register for; Pending are awaiting an approve/reject decision; Completed are events whose date has passed. Each has its own carousel and list.
How is event attendance tracked?
From Event Details: the Registrations tab shows who signed up, and on or after event day the Attendance section records who actually attended. Attendance feeds the analytics.
What does the Mobility Program Admin do?
The Mobility Program Admin is International Office staff responsible for day-to-day academic mobility operations: reviewing applications, approving or rejecting students, nominating approved students to partner universities, and maintaining mobility programs, partner universities, documents, reports, settings, and notifications.
What does the Mobility Program paid feature unlock?
It unlocks the mobility command center, application review, approval queue, nomination hub, program catalog, partner university directory, admin document view, operational reports, and the dedicated Mobility Program Admin role. It does not change the Student guide in this update.
What can the University Super Admin do?
The University Super Admin runs one university end to end. You manage all the people — inviting students, ambassadors, external ambassadors, and community and event coordinators, assigning their roles, and suspending accounts when needed. You configure the university itself — branding, forms, notification preferences, and language — and you share documents with students one at a time or in bulk through Take Action. You resolve reports and own moderation across the tenant. For oversight you can open any other role's dashboard read-only — Student, Ambassador, Community or Event Coordinator, even Third-Party Coordinator — to see exactly what they see, while the actions stay theirs.
Who can invite or suspend users?
The University Super Admin. Coordinators have focused tools (events, communities, assignments) but inviting and suspending users sits with the Super Admin. User invites also require the User Management module to be Active and within licensed capacity.
Where do I see which features our university has purchased?
INTOTO WebApp → My University → Features tab. Each module shows Active status, contract dates, and (for User Management) seat utilization. Full module descriptions are in the Paid Features catalog.
What's the difference between a coordinator and the Super Admin?
Coordinators own one slice — events, communities, or partner assignments. The Super Admin owns the whole university: people, all queues, and governance. Coordinators can't cross into each other's domains; the Super Admin oversees them all.

Partner network

What is a Third Party Coordinator?
An external partner — typically a travel, relocation, or arrival-services organisation — that receives a pool of students from a university and gets them from arrival to campus by delegating them to its own sub-coordinators.
How does assigning students work?
The coordinator selects students from the pool and runs the Assign Students setup: pick a sub-coordinator, set a from/to service window, write a work brief, choose which fields are visible, then confirm — assigning the whole batch in one step.
What is field-level visibility?
In the assignment setup the coordinator toggles which student fields a sub-coordinator may see. Only the enabled fields are shared — data-minimisation by default, so field staff see exactly what they need and nothing more.
What does a Third Party Sub Coordinator do?
They're the field operator — greeter, driver, or host — who works the students assigned to them, reads each arrival brief, meets the student, and records how the arrival went.
What arrival statuses can a student have?
A placed student moves through Assigned → Attended → Completed as the sub-coordinator updates it. If a pickup didn't happen it's marked Not Attended with a reason. The status and reason show on the coordinator's Arrival Request Details.
What reasons can a sub-coordinator give for Not Attended?
Student not answering phone, Flight delayed, Student arrived late, Incorrect arrival details, or Other. Choosing Other requires a note; notes are optional for the other reasons.
How do reassign and unassign requests work?
A sub-coordinator who can't take a student raises an unassign or reassign request. It reaches the assigning coordinator's Notifications, who accepts or declines — the student stays on the sub-coordinator's list until they act.
Can a sub-coordinator reassign students themselves?
No. Only the Third Party Coordinator can reassign or remove. The sub-coordinator can request it, but the actual move — and choosing who it goes to — stays with the coordinator so load is managed centrally.

Community creation workflow

The Community module separates trusted staff creation from user-submitted creation requests. A community is only discoverable and joinable after it becomes Active.

Coordinator / Super Admin creation

  1. Community Coordinator (or University Super Admin on web) creates a community on a topic.
  2. The community publishes instantly — no pending approval step.
  3. It becomes visible to relevant roles (Students, Third-Party Coordinators, and staff who can browse communities).

Student or Third-Party Coordinator request

  1. Student or Third-Party Coordinator submits a request to create a community.
  2. The request lands in the pending queue for Community Coordinator / University Super Admin.
  3. Until approval, the community remains in Pending state: only the creator and approving coordinator/admin can see it; other users cannot search, view, or join it.
  4. Approved — community is created and visible to relevant users; requester is notified.
  5. Rejected — community does not go live; requester receives a notification with the rejection reason.

Common questions

Intoto Admin FAQs

The questions admins, support, sales, and demo audiences ask most often about the platform role.

What can the Intoto Admin do?
Run the whole platform — onboard and manage universities, invite and control users across every tenant, and oversee communities, events, and reports. It's the top administrator role above any single university.
How is the Intoto Admin different from a University Super Admin?
Scope. The University Super Admin runs one university; the Intoto Admin operates across all universities, manages tenants themselves, and can switch university context. Only the Intoto Admin can add, suspend, or activate a university.
How do I onboard a new university?
Use the Add University quick action: enter the institution name, optional logo, identifier/domain, primary contact, and an initial campus, then optionally invite the first University Super Admin and submit. The tenant appears in the University List.
Can I focus on just one university?
Yes. Use the university context switcher in the header (or the University filter on any list) to scope counts and lists to one tenant, then select All Universities to return to the platform-wide view.
Which roles can I invite?
Any invitable role, including University Super Admin. A University is required for every university-scoped invite; a campus is required for operational roles but skipped for a Super Admin.
Do I assign students to coordinators?
Typically no — that's a University Super Admin or coordinator operation. The Intoto Admin has read oversight of student lists and profiles for volume and compliance, and reviews assignment status rather than running the assign wizard.
How do I handle moderation across tenants?
Open Total Reports, filter by university if needed, and move each case through Assigned → Under Review → Escalated → Resolved. My Assigned Reports gives you a personal queue.
What are the Quick Way cards, and what happens when I click one?
Quick Way cards are the stat tiles in the Dashboard Overview band — Students, Ambassadors, External Ambassadors, Third-Party Coordinators, Sub-coordinators, Event Coordinators, Community Coordinators, and University Super Admin. Each shows a live count, and clicking a card opens its own dedicated dashboard for that group, with its own list, stats, and actions.
What does my dashboard show?
A Dashboard Overview band of Quick Way stat cards plus analytics widgets — Student Admission, Student's Location, Communities, Event Status Distribution, Student Travel Plan, and Message — with a university filter, a date range, and per-widget export in the right rail.
Can one person be both an Intoto Admin and something else?
Yes. Multi-role accounts pick a role at Role Selection on login, or switch later from the role switcher; Home and tabs reload for whichever role is active.
What paid features can Intoto provide to a university?
Seven standalone modules: Ambassador Management, Travel Plan, Community, Chat, Event Management, User Management, and Mobility Program. A university can buy one, several, or all — see the Paid Features catalog for full detail on each.
Who provisions paid features for a university?
The Intoto Admin during university onboarding or when editing a tenant — setting Active state, contract dates, and User Management capacity. The University Super Admin sees the result read-only on My University → Features but cannot change subscriptions.
Can a university add features after go-live?
Yes. Intoto Admin enables additional modules when sales/onboarding requests it. Each new module gets its own Start/End dates and unlocks the related admin and student surfaces for that tenant only.
What happens if a module is not provisioned?
That functionality is hidden for the tenant — no related sidebar entries, dashboard cards, or student experiences. For example, without Chat provisioned, the Chats tab does not appear for that university's users.
How does User Management capacity work?
Intoto Admin sets Total Capacity and optional domestic/international limits when provisioning User Management. The university's Features tab shows live utilization; invites are blocked when capacity is exhausted until the limit is increased.

Ambassador FAQs

The questions ambassadors, support, and demo audiences ask most often about the role.

What’s the difference between an Ambassador and an External Ambassador?
An Ambassador is usually a current student or alumnus (often also holding the Student role); an External Ambassador is an off-campus partner invited with the External ambassador type. Both use the same flows and profile form.
Can I be both a Student and an Ambassador?
Yes — that’s common. You choose which context to work in at login or via the role switcher, and the app keeps the two cleanly separate.
How do I switch between my Student and Ambassador roles?
Use the role switcher on Home or Profile, or pick the role at login. Home and the tabs reload for whichever role you choose.
Why is completing my profile so important?
Your Ambassador Info and photo are exactly what students see on the Ambassadors carousel. Incomplete profiles are hidden or ranked lower, so completeness is how students find and trust you.

The public, mentor-facing part of your profile: bio, type, programs, languages, availability, and interests. Everything students see on your card comes from here.

In Chats. A student taps your card on their home carousel, opens your profile, and taps Chat — the conversation lands in your Chats inbox.
Can I message lots of students at once?
No. Ambassador messaging is one-to-one and support-oriented; mass communication to the student body is a staff capability.

No. You participate — register for events, join and post in communities — but approvals belong to the Event and Community Coordinator roles.

Common questions

Community Coordinator FAQs

The questions coordinators, support, and demo audiences ask most often about the role.

What is the Community Coordinator responsible for?
Reviewing, publishing, and moderating communities within one university — clearing the pending queue and keeping student groups safe and on-policy.
How does community approval work?
New communities arrive in a pending queue. You review each one and approve it to publish or reject it with a reason. Students only ever see approved communities.
What happens when I reject a community?
It doesn’t go live, and the reason you give explains what needs to change. Rejected communities leave the active queue.
How do I handle reported posts or content?
Reported content surfaces in a moderation queue where you can review it and take action according to policy.
Can I create or approve events?
No — events belong to the Event Coordinator. You focus on communities; you can browse events like any member.
Can I invite or suspend users?
No. Inviting and suspending users is the University Super Admin’s job. Your tools are community-focused.
What’s the difference between me and the University Super Admin?
You own the community slice; the Super Admin owns the whole university — people, all queues, and governance — and can step into any area.
Do students see pending communities?
No. Only approved communities appear to students, which is exactly why the approval gate matters.
Am I limited to one university?
Yes — each role assignment is scoped to one university (and campus where configured).
What can students do in Community?
Submit a creation request (pending your approval), join approved communities, post in communities they have joined, see feeds with the latest post, like/comment/share, start chat, and view profiles. They never see unapproved communities in discovery.
What can Third-Party Coordinators do?
Same participation model as students — request creation (pending approval), join, post, engage — plus join suggestions for communities relevant to their interests or role.
Who gets notified about community activity?
Users are notified when a new community becomes available, when someone posts in a community they joined, and when their own creation request is approved or rejected (rejection includes the reason).
Can there be more than one Community Coordinator?
Yes. A university can run a team of any number of Community Coordinators sharing the pending queue, moderation, and official community creation.

Common questions

University Super Admin FAQs

What can the University Super Admin do?
Run one university on the web portal and mobile app — manage users, clear profile review, edit university and campus content, configure forms and permissions, share documents, resolve reports, and oversee travel, ambassadors, and events. On mobile you can also open every other role's dashboard read-only for oversight.
How is University Super Admin different from Intoto Admin?
Scope. The University Super Admin runs one university. The Intoto Admin operates across all universities, onboards tenants, and can switch university context. Only the Intoto Admin can add, suspend, or activate a university.
Do I use web, mobile, or both?
Both are the same role. Use the web portal for bulk operations — user tables, profile review, campus CRUD, My University content, the form builder, and event publishing. Use the mobile app for pulse checks, chat, read-only coordinator oversight, and on-the-go account actions.
Who can invite or suspend users?
The University Super Admin. Coordinators have focused tools but inviting and suspending users sits with the Super Admin.
How do I invite a new user?
Use Add User / Add Users, choose the role, fill required fields (campus except for Super Admin invites), and submit. For bulk invites on web, use file import and track progress in Invite Data.
Why can't students fully onboard?
Their profile may be in Profile Review (web). Filter Pending Review, approve or reject each student, and counts update as you work through the backlog.
What is the difference between My University and Configurations?
My University is the content students read — about text, programs, admissions copy — and the Features tab shows which paid modules are active for the tenant. Configurations is the schema — which sections and fields exist, validation rules, and per-role permissions.
How do paid features work — can we buy only some modules?
Yes. Intoto modules are standalone subscriptions — a university can take one feature, several, or all seven (Ambassador Management, Travel Plan, Community, Chat, Event Management, User Management, Mobility Program) and add more at any time. The My University → Features tab lists each module with Active status and contract dates; User Management also shows total capacity, utilisation, and domestic vs international seat counts. Provisioning is done by Intoto Admin; the Super Admin uses this tab to see what is enabled. See also the Paid Features catalog.
Who turns features on for our university?
Intoto Admin provisions modules when your institution is onboarded or when your account team requests a change. You see the result on My University → Features — you cannot enable or disable modules from this screen.
What happens if a feature is not Active?
That module's admin tools and student experiences are hidden. For example, without Community active there is no Community sidebar or student group creation; without Chat, the mobile Chats tab does not appear.
What does the User Management capacity on the Features tab mean?
Total Capacity is your licensed seat pool. Utilization shows how many seats are used (percentage and count). Domestic and International rows show split limits if your contract defines them. You cannot invite beyond remaining capacity until Intoto increases the limit.
Can we add Travel Plan or Events later?
Yes. Universities often start with User Management and Chat, then add Travel Plan before intake, Event Management when programming scales, or Mobility Program when the international office wants application review, nomination, partner, program, and reporting workflows in Intoto. Each new module appears on the Features tab with its own dates once Intoto provisions it.
Can I send documents to students?
Yes. Share Document on a student profile for one recipient, or select several students → Take Action → Share Document for bulk. Files appear under Shared by University on both views.
Can I see what other roles are doing?
Yes — user by user. From a role's Quick Way card open the people list, tap a name, and their own dashboard opens read-only. Third-Party Sub Coordinators are not accessible.
Do I approve communities and events on every surface?
On web, yes — pending communities and the event lifecycle are actionable here. On mobile, you view coordinator dashboards and queues read-only; use web for approvals or coordinate with the Community / Event Coordinator.
Do I assign students to sub-coordinators?
No. That flow belongs to the Third Party Coordinator. The University Super Admin has no access to Third-Party Sub Coordinators.
What does the dashboard show?
On web, ten Dashboard Overview stat cards plus admission, travel, location, and community analytics. On mobile, governance-oriented Quick Way cards and quick actions in backend-defined order.

Who the Third Party Coordinator is

The Third Party Coordinator is an external partner — typically a travel, relocation, or arrival-services organisation — engaged by a university to receive incoming students and get them safely from arrival to campus. Inside Intoto they hold a pool of students, delegate batches to their own sub-coordinators with a service window and clear instructions, control exactly which student details each sub-coordinator can see, and watch every assignment move through its arrival states. They are not university administrators — they don't invite arbitrary users, approve events, or moderate communities.

The one-paragraph version

A coordinator works a pool of assigned students, runs the Assign Students flow (pick a sub-coordinator, set the date range, write the work brief, choose which fields are visible, then select students), then tracks each arrival through Assigned → Attended → Completed — handling reassign, remove, and unassign requests along the way, all scoped to their university.

What is the Third Party Coordinator responsible for?

Who the Sub Coordinator is

08

Moderation — report & delete

Safety lives in the three-dot menu on every chat header. Visitors can report inappropriate conversations or delete a thread; destructive actions always require a confirmation step.

  1. In any individual or group chat, tap the ⋮ menu on the header.
  2. Choose Report a conversation — fill the reason in the modal and submit.
  3. Or choose Delete conversation — confirm in the popup to remove the thread.
  4. After report, the after report confirmation UI shows the action recorded.
  5. Cancel from any modal returns to the chat with no state change.
Product

Both actions sit inside the chat header so they're contextual and obvious — visitors don't hunt through settings to feel safe.

QA focus

Both actions appear on every chat header; Report submits and shows the after-report state; Delete removes the thread from the list; cancel does nothing destructive.

Marketing

"Universities get peer mentorship without losing safety — report and moderation are built in."

Common questions

Ambassador Platform FAQs

The questions universities, prospects, support, and demo audiences ask most often about the platform.

What is The Ambassador Platform?
A public-facing web portal where prospective and incoming students discover real university ambassadors, read profiles, and chat one-to-one or in cohort groups. It's branded Student Ambassadors with Powered by Intoto in the header, and universities embed it on their own site.
How is this different from the Intoto mobile app?
The platform is a browser product focused on discovery and chat — meeting ambassadors before applying or enrolling. The mobile app is the full student lifecycle after enrolment: profile, travel, communities, events, and university admin. Different audience, different surface, different scope.
How do External Ambassadors appear on the platform?
Off-campus partners invited as External Ambassador show in the directory with a Staff badge (Figma Employee filter). Prospects filter by Staff, open the card, and tap Chat; the partner replies from the External Ambassador web portal. See Intoto External Ambassador Feature & Flow Guide for the fulfilment side.
Do visitors need to be enrolled students to use it?
No. The platform is built for prospective and incoming students — anyone can sign up with email and password, verify, and start chatting. Universities embed it to convert interest into conversations long before an application is in.
How does a visitor find an ambassador who matches them?
Two ways: search by name, program, or country in the search bar, or use the filter chips — Available Now, Students, Alumni, Staff, By Program, By Country. Active filters refresh the card grid; By Program and By Country open a picker first.
What's the difference between an Individual Chat and a Group Chat?
Individual chat is a one-to-one thread with a specific ambassador. Group chats are cohort or program rooms (e.g. International Students 2025) where many members and ambassadors talk together. Both live in the same Chat surface, behind two tabs.
What does the green Online dot mean?
The ambassador is currently available. It's a soft signal — even offline ambassadors usually respond within their stated response time — but Online suggests a real-time reply is likely.
Who moderates conversations?
The platform offers a per-chat report and delete via the three-dot menu, and the university's Intoto Admin and Super Admin handle moderation in the admin tools. Reports surface to those roles; visitors don't see moderation queues.
Can visitors send images in chat?
Yes — the composer has an attachment icon for image uploads, and group chats include a shared media gallery. Other file types are out of scope for this product surface.
What happens if a visitor doesn't verify their email?
The Verification Required! screen blocks the rest of the product until they click the link sent to their inbox. Login also redirects unverified accounts back to this gate, so chat and the directory require a verified address.
Can the platform be white-labelled?
In the current Figma scope, Powered by Intoto appears in the header alongside the university's Student Ambassadors branding. Full white-label is a product-roadmap conversation.
How do visitors delete their data?
From the profile icon → Delete Account. The flow confirms the destructive action, removes the account, and the email cannot log back in — a self-serve route designed with GDPR-style expectations in mind.
Why does the design specify three breakpoints?
International prospects browse on whatever they have — laptops at home, tablets at school, phones in transit. 1920, 1280, and 412 cover the realistic top, middle, and floor of that range without micro-optimising for every device.
