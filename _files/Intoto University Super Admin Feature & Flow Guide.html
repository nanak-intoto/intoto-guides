At a glance

Who the University Super Admin is

The University Super Admin is the highest operational administrator within one university. They manage people — staff, coordinators, students, and ambassadors — configure the tenant, share documents with students, and resolve reports. On the INTOTO WebApp they run bulk operations from a twelve-module sidebar: dashboard metrics, user lifecycle, profile review, university and campus content, communities, travel, ambassadors, events, chat, and the form builder. On the mobile app they use a four-tab layout for the same governance scope plus read-only oversight of every other role's dashboard. They never pick a campus at login on mobile — the app already knows the institution. They do not manage other universities; that is the Intoto Admin.

The one-paragraph version

A University Super Admin signs into the web portal or mobile app, reads live university health from dashboard stat cards, invites and manages users, clears the Profile Review queue on web, keeps My University content current, adjusts Configurations, shares documents with students, and triages reports. On mobile they can also open any other role's dashboard read-only — user by user — without performing that role's operational actions.

A day in the role

How the work flows

A typical loop — read the state of the university, act on people and content that need attention, then keep everyone moving. Most heavy operations happen on web; mobile is for pulse checks, chat, and read-only oversight.

1

Sign in

Web: open the WebApp URL → Continue on Login or Sign-up → SSO → land on Dashboard with sidebar. Mobile: email/password or SSO → single role lands on Home; multi-role picks University Super Admin first.

2

Read the pulse

Scan dashboard stat cards and analytics widgets — students, communities, travel, events, reports. Tap any number to drill into the list behind it.

3

Clear onboarding backlog

On web, open Profile Review → filter Pending Review → approve or reject student profiles. In Manage User, invite coordinators or approve Invited rows.

4

Govern content & settings

Update My University copy, add a Campus if needed, and adjust Configurations (forms, permissions, feature flags).

5

Moderate & oversee

On web, approve pending communities and move events through create → published; triage Reports. On mobile, open coordinator dashboards read-only to audit volumes without acting in their tools.

6

Stay connected

Chat with students and staff; send bulk email from user lists; share documents with one student or a cohort.

Features

Intoto paid features — standalone modules

Intoto is sold as standalone paid features, not as a single all-or-nothing platform license. A university can subscribe to one module, several, or all six, and can add modules at any time as its needs grow — for example User Management and Chat in year one, then Travel Plan and Ambassador Management before the next intake. For the full product catalog with per-module detail, see the Intoto Paid Features Guide.

The University Super Admin sees every subscribed module on the My University → Features tab in the web portal. Each module appears as its own card with a short description, an Active (or inactive) badge, and a contract window (Start Date / End Date). The Super Admin does not turn modules on or off from this screen — Intoto provisions subscriptions during commercial onboarding — but this tab is the source of truth for what the portal and mobile app will expose to students and staff.

  1. Sidebar → My University → Features tab.
  2. Review each card — title, description, Active status, and subscription dates.
  3. For User Management, also read Total Capacity, Utilization, and domestic vs international seat counts before bulk invites.
  4. Confirm related sidebar modules (Community, Chat, Travel Plan, etc.) match the active cards before promising capabilities to stakeholders.

Getting in

Sign-in uses university credentials (Auth0 / SSO). Multi-role accounts choose University Super Admin at role selection. There is no campus picker — the app already knows the institution.

  1. Web: open the INTOTO WebApp URL → on Login or Sign-up tap Continue → complete SSO on the identity provider.
  2. Mobile: open the Intoto app → enter email/password or use configured SSO.
  3. Single role → land on Dashboard (web) or Home (mobile).
  4. Multi-role → Role Selection appears; choose University Super Admin (or switch later from profile / dashboard role change on web).
  5. Web: sidebar loads with university logo; header shows date, greeting, language, notifications, and role badge.
  6. Mobile: four bottom tabs appear (Home, Community, Chats, Profile).
Product

One role, two surfaces — SSO on both, conditional role selection only for multi-hat accounts, and no campus picker because the Super Admin operates above campus level.

QA focus

Single-role lands on admin dashboard (not student home); multi-role shows Role Selection; web sidebar highlights active module; mobile tab set loads after login and after role switch.

Marketing

"One secure sign-in opens your university's command center — in the browser or on your phone."

03

Manage users

The account-control hub for everyone in the university — filterable by status, with actions that depend on where each person sits in the invitation lifecycle. On web this is a full data table; on mobile, status chips and row actions serve the same lifecycle.

  1. Open Manage User from the sidebar (web) or a quick action / stat card (mobile).
  2. Filter by status — All, Invited, Accepted, Withdrawn, Rejected, Suspended — or use Filter → Campus and/or Role → Apply.
  3. Search by name or email.
  4. Tap a row for per-person actions, or enter selection mode for bulk email.
  5. Web: use row ⋮ → Approve, Reject, or Download; paginate with Showing 1–10 controls.

04

Add user & invites

A single invite form sends platform invitations by email. Fields adapt to the role; campus is required for operational roles but skipped when inviting a peer Super Admin. On web, Add Users in Manage User and Invite Data track bulk imports alongside single invites.

  1. Open Add User / Add Users from the dashboard quick action or Manage User toolbar.
  2. Select the role (Student, Ambassador, External Ambassador, coordinators, University Super Admin, etc.).
  3. Enter one email, or import a file for bulk invites (Invite Data on web tracks upload status).
  4. Select campus — required for most roles; skipped for University Super Admin.
  5. Set role-specific fields: Ambassador Type, profile visibility, invite type (students).
  6. Pick an expiry date → Submit → row appears as Invited in Manage User.

07

Students & share documents

Find any student in the university, open a deep read-only profile, and share documents — one at a time from a profile or in bulk from a list. Every shared file lands in the student's Shared by University section. Assigning students to sub-coordinators is not this role's flow (Third Party Coordinator).

  1. Open All Students / Total Students from a stat card or Manage User filters.
  2. Search (3+ characters on web lists) or filter by campus, program, country, etc.
  3. Tap a row → Student Profile Hub — sections and travel data, read-only.
  4. One student: profile → Share Document → upload → Send.
  5. Many students: list → selection mode → Take Action → Share Document.
  6. Confirm the file appears under Shared by University on both admin and student views.
Product

Read-and-support, not delegation — one sharing model with two entry points, both writing to the same predictable section on the student profile.

QA focus

Directory and profile open read-only; no Assign / sub-coordinator wizard; single- and bulk-share post to correct recipients; Third-Party Sub Coordinators unreachable.

Marketing

"Find any student in seconds and get the right documents into their hands — to one or a whole intake."

08

Communities & reports

Communities and reports share a moderation mindset but differ by surface. On web, the Super Admin approves pending community requests and triages reports. On mobile, the same role opens coordinator dashboards and queues read-only — approvals happen on web or stay with the Community Coordinator when acting through mobile oversight views.

  1. Web: Sidebar → Community → Pending Requests → approve or deny each request.
  2. Web: switch to Active Communities → open feed → Create post or triage Reports by status.
  3. Mobile: Community tab or stat card → view communities and pending queue without Approve/Reject controls.
  4. For reports: tap Total Reports → move cases through Assigned → Under Review → Escalated → Resolved.

Surface note: Report resolution is owned by the University Super Admin on both web and mobile. Community approval is actionable on the web portal; on mobile the Super Admin monitors volumes and escalates to the Community Coordinator when action is needed.

Product

Separation of duties on mobile keeps coordinator domains intact; web gives the Super Admin direct gates where bulk operational throughput matters.

QA focus

Web approve/deny updates Active list; mobile has no community status mutations; report transitions persist; My vs Assigned report views scope correctly.

Marketing

"Keep student groups safe — approve communities on web, resolve reports everywhere, and see the full picture on mobile."

Events

On web, the Super Admin runs the full event lifecycle — create, approve, publish, register, attend, and collect feedback. On mobile, events open read-only through the Event Coordinator's catalog and pending queue; creating and approving stays on web or with the Event Coordinator.

  1. Web: Dashboard → Events → Create Event → submit or save Draft → Assign Co-ordinator if required → approve → Published Event Detail.
  2. Review Registration list / Attendees → on event day Mark Present → collect Feedback.
  3. Mobile: Events quick action or carousel → browse catalog and Pending Events read-only.

Surface note: Event creation and approval on web; mobile is oversight and analytics, not the primary publishing surface.

Product

The pending → published gate on web keeps off-policy events off the student calendar until an admin approves them; mobile visibility supports audit without duplicating the coordinator's publish tools.

QA focus

Web create → pending → published path persists; mobile has no Approve/Reject/Create; Mark Present and feedback captured post-event.

Marketing

"Events move from draft to published to attended — with registration, check-in, and feedback built in on web."

Oversight model

Cross-role visibility

From a role's Quick Way card the Super Admin opens that role's people list, taps a name, and that person's own role dashboard opens — with their name — read-only. Their own powers (account management, reports, document sharing, configuration) stay separate.

Role card on the dashboardTap a person → their own dashboard shows
StudentsThat student's onboarding, profile, and travel progress
AmbassadorsThat ambassador's profile, activity, and conversation counts
External AmbassadorsThat external ambassador's profile and activity
Community CoordinatorsThe communities and pending items that coordinator handles
Event CoordinatorsThe events that coordinator runs and their analytics
Third-Party CoordinatorsWhat that coordinator has done with their assigned student pool

Out of scope: Third-Party Sub Coordinators are not accessible. Student-to-sub-coordinator assignment belongs to the Third Party Coordinator, not this role.

Product

One administrator gets a complete, auditable picture without inheriting every role's job — visibility is broad; authority is scoped.

QA focus

From a role card, tap a user and confirm their dashboard opens read-only with their name; action controls absent or disabled; no Super-Admin interaction mutates another user's records.

Marketing

"Total oversight, zero overreach — see how every part of your university is running, with each job kept in the right hands."

Common questions

University Super Admin FAQs

What can the University Super Admin do?
Run one university on the web portal and mobile app — manage users, clear profile review, edit university and campus content, configure forms and permissions, share documents, resolve reports, and oversee travel, ambassadors, and events. On mobile you can also open every other role's dashboard read-only for oversight.
How is University Super Admin different from Intoto Admin?
Scope. The University Super Admin runs one university. The Intoto Admin operates across all universities, onboards tenants, and can switch university context. Only the Intoto Admin can add, suspend, or activate a university.
Do I use web, mobile, or both?
Both are the same role. Use the web portal for bulk operations — user tables, profile review, campus CRUD, My University content, the form builder, and event publishing. Use the mobile app for pulse checks, chat, read-only coordinator oversight, and on-the-go account actions.
Who can invite or suspend users?
The University Super Admin. Coordinators have focused tools but inviting and suspending users sits with the Super Admin.
How do I invite a new user?
Use Add User / Add Users, choose the role, fill required fields (campus except for Super Admin invites), and submit. For bulk invites on web, use file import and track progress in Invite Data.
Why can't students fully onboard?
Their profile may be in Profile Review (web). Filter Pending Review, approve or reject each student, and counts update as you work through the backlog.
What is the difference between My University and Configurations?
My University is the content students read — about text, programs, admissions copy — and the Features tab shows which paid modules are active for the tenant. Configurations is the schema — which sections and fields exist, validation rules, and per-role permissions.
How do paid features work — can we buy only some modules?
Yes. Intoto modules are standalone subscriptions — a university can take one feature, several, or all six (Ambassador Management, Travel Plan, Community, Chat, Event Management, User Management) and add more at any time. The My University → Features tab lists each module with Active status and contract dates; User Management also shows total capacity, utilisation, and domestic vs international seat counts. Provisioning is done by Intoto Admin; the Super Admin uses this tab to see what is enabled. See also the Paid Features catalog.
Who turns features on for our university?
Intoto Admin provisions modules when your institution is onboarded or when your account team requests a change. You see the result on My University → Features — you cannot enable or disable modules from this screen.
What happens if a feature is not Active?
That module's admin tools and student experiences are hidden. For example, without Community active there is no Community sidebar or student group creation; without Chat, the mobile Chats tab does not appear.
What does the User Management capacity on the Features tab mean?
Total Capacity is your licensed seat pool. Utilization shows how many seats are used (percentage and count). Domestic and International rows show split limits if your contract defines them. You cannot invite beyond remaining capacity until Intoto increases the limit.
Can we add Travel Plan or Events later?
Yes. Universities often start with User Management and Chat, then add Travel Plan before intake or Event Management when programming scales. Each new module appears on the Features tab with its own dates once Intoto provisions it.
Can I send documents to students?
Yes. Share Document on a student profile for one recipient, or select several students → Take Action → Share Document for bulk. Files appear under Shared by University on both views.
Can I see what other roles are doing?
Yes — user by user. From a role's Quick Way card open the people list, tap a name, and their own dashboard opens read-only. Third-Party Sub Coordinators are not accessible.
Do I approve communities and events on every surface?
On web, yes — pending communities and the event lifecycle are actionable here. On mobile, you view coordinator dashboards and queues read-only; use web for approvals or coordinate with the Community / Event Coordinator.
Do I assign students to sub-coordinators?
No. That flow belongs to the Third Party Coordinator. The University Super Admin has no access to Third-Party Sub Coordinators.
What does the dashboard show?
On web, ten Dashboard Overview stat cards plus admission, travel, location, and community analytics. On mobile, governance-oriented Quick Way cards and quick actions in backend-defined order.
