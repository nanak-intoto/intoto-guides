At a glance

What The Ambassador Platform is

A standalone web portal — branded Student Ambassadors with Powered by Intoto in the header — that universities embed so a prospect or incoming student can find a real mentor and start a conversation in minutes. Visitors sign up, verify email, create a basic profile, then browse ambassadors with search and filters, read full profiles, and message ambassadors one-to-one or join program and cohort group chats. It is intentionally narrow: discovery and chat. Operational tools — approvals, governance, tenant configuration — live elsewhere in the Intoto mobile app and admin roles.

The one-paragraph version

A prospect lands on All Ambassadors, filters by program or country, opens an ambassador's profile, taps Chat, and gets a real answer from a real student — all from the browser, all before they download anything. The platform also runs Group Chats for cohorts and a public Ambassador FAQ for visitors who aren't ready to message yet.

The visitor's journey

From URL to first chat

The shortest path from a curious prospect to a live conversation with an ambassador is five steps — and they happen entirely in the browser.

1

Sign up

Open the platform, tap Signup, enter email and password. Optional Remember me; Forget password is one tap away.

2

Verify email

The Verification Required! screen confirms the address used; the visitor follows the link sent to their inbox.

3

Create profile

Fill Basic Information — photo (optional), name, email, phone with country code, country — then Submit.

4

Browse & filter

Land on All Ambassadors, search by name/program/country, or use chips like Available Now, Students, Alumni, Staff, By Program, By Country.

5

Read & chat

Open a card to read About me, languages, programs, response time — then tap Chat to start the conversation, or join a Group Chat for the cohort.

Structure

How the site is organised

The chrome is consistent across every signed-in screen: a header with the page title, the chat icon, the profile icon, and Powered by Intoto. The body is built around four ideas.

Surface 01
All Ambassadors

The directory home — search, filter chips, card grids (Chat with Our Ambassadors, Ambassadors Card Final), See More, and the FAQ section.

Surface 02
Ambassadors Profile

The full profile detail page — detail panel on the left (photo, badge, Chat CTA, program, country, languages, response time) and About me / Ask me a question on the right.

Surface 03
Chat

Two-column inbox on desktop — Individual Chats and Group Chats tabs on the left, the active conversation on the right with composer and quick-reply chips.

Surface 04
Account

01

Sign up, log in, verify

Onboarding is intentionally lightweight: an email and a password to start, an email verification to build trust, and a returning-user login that lands on the directory. The product nudges prospects past the form and into the people as fast as possible.

  1. Open Signup. Enter email and password; optionally tick Remember Me.
  2. Tap Continue. A loader resolves the API call.
  3. If unverified, the Verification Required! card shows the email used and tells the visitor to check their inbox.
  4. Returning users sign in on Login; an unverified login is bounced back to the verification card.
  5. Forget Password? opens the password reset flow.
Product

Email-and-password keeps the bar low for prospects who have not yet applied — university SSO is reserved for the Intoto mobile app, where it makes sense.

QA focus

Empty/invalid email and weak password are blocked inline; Remember Me persists across sessions; the verification card displays the submitted email; Already have an account? Log in swaps to Login; Forget Password is not a dead link.

Marketing

"Two fields and an email link — sign up and you're talking to an ambassador the same minute."

02

Create profile — Basic Information

After verification the visitor sets up a minimal identity so ambassadors know who they're talking to. The form is short by design: photo, name, email, phone with country code, and country. Chat — not data capture — is the goal.

  1. Land on Create profile · Basic Information.
  2. Optionally tap the circular avatar to upload a picture.
  3. Fill First Name, Last Name, Email ID, Phone (with country code), Country.
  4. Tap Submit.
  5. Land on All Ambassadors — the directory home.
Product

Profile is minimal because every extra field is a drop-off; ambassadors don't need ten fields to start a conversation.

QA focus

Required fields block Submit; photo preview persists to View Profile; phone code + national number store as a single E.164-style value; country dropdown is searchable.

Marketing

"A short form, not a survey — your profile is just enough for an ambassador to greet you by name."

04

The ambassador card

A card is the atomic unit of the directory and the place where a prospect's interest converts into a chat. It's small on purpose: a face, a status, a role, a few words, and a button.

  1. Scan the status row: Online green dot and role badge (Student, Alumni, Staff).
  2. Read the circular photo (with country flag overlay), name, and program subtitle.
  3. Read the bio snippet — long bios truncate with ellipsis on some layouts.
  4. Tap Chat to open a thread with this ambassador.
  5. Tap the card body or photo to open the full Ambassadors Profile.
Product

Two intentional taps — Chat for the impatient, the card body for the curious — keeps the card simple while serving both.

QA focus

The Online dot reflects real availability; the role badge matches the ambassador's record; tap targets are large enough on mobile; Chat opens the correct thread (new or existing).

Marketing

"Online ambassadors get a green dot — proof a real person is on the other side, right now."

05

Ambassador profile — full page

The profile is where a prospect decides whether to message. The left panel carries the trust signals — photo, badge, program, country, languages, favourite programs, response time — and the right side carries the story.

  1. Open the profile by tapping a card body in the directory.
  2. Read the left detail panel: photo, name, role badge, short description, Chat CTA, Program, Country, Languages, Favourite Programs, response-time hint (e.g. Usually responds within 24 hours).
  3. Read the About me long bio on the right.
  4. Tap Ask me a question for a guided chat entry.
  5. Tap Chat on the left panel to open the thread.
Product

Profiles are ambassador-authored — the voice is honest, not marketing copy — which is the whole point of peer mentorship.

QA focus

All detail panel fields render when data exists and degrade cleanly when empty; About me is fully readable without unwanted truncation; Ask me a question routes to a chat entry; the gallery and Like/share controls behave per design.

Marketing

"Read their story first — languages they speak, programs they love, how fast they reply — before you ever say hello."

07

Group chat

Group chats turn the platform into a peer community. Cohorts like International Students 2025 and program groups give a whole intake a single shared room — searchable, moderated, and persistent.

  1. Open Chat and switch to the Group Chats tab.
  2. Tap a group (e.g. International Students 2025, Business Administration Students, Campus Events & Activities).
  3. Read the thread — each message shows the sender's name and role badge.
  4. Tap the header to open Group Detail — member list and settings.
  5. Use Mute to silence notifications or Leave Group to exit (with confirmation).
  6. Tap shared images to open the gallery viewer.
Product

Groups carry cohort identity — joining one is the platform's way of saying "you belong with these people," before classes even start.

QA focus

Tab switch preserves state; group headers show correct member count; sender names and badges render per message; Mute and Leave Group are reversible until Leave is confirmed; after exit, the group disappears from the list.

Marketing

"One room for your whole intake — meet classmates before the welcome week."

08

Moderation — report & delete

Safety lives in the three-dot menu on every chat header. Visitors can report inappropriate conversations or delete a thread; destructive actions always require a confirmation step.

  1. In any individual or group chat, tap the ⋮ menu on the header.
  2. Choose Report a conversation — fill the reason in the modal and submit.
  3. Or choose Delete conversation — confirm in the popup to remove the thread.
  4. After report, the after report confirmation UI shows the action recorded.
  5. Cancel from any modal returns to the chat with no state change.
Product

Both actions sit inside the chat header so they're contextual and obvious — visitors don't hunt through settings to feel safe.

QA focus

Both actions appear on every chat header; Report submits and shows the after-report state; Delete removes the thread from the list; cancel does nothing destructive.

Marketing

"Universities get peer mentorship without losing safety — report and moderation are built in."

09

Ambassador FAQ

Below the card grid on the directory home, the Ambassador FAQ is ambassador-authored, evergreen content — useful for prospects not ready to message and for SEO. Each row is a familiar accordion.

  1. Scroll past the card grids on All Ambassadors to Ambassador FAQ.
  2. Tap a collapsed question row — the answer expands.
  3. Tap an open row again, or another row, to switch.
Product

FAQ content does double duty — it answers prospects who never chat, and it indexes for search engines bringing them in.

QA focus

Accordion expand/collapse without layout shift; long answers don't break the page; behaviour matches spec (single-open vs multi-open).

Marketing

"Real answers from real ambassadors — not a chatbot, not a brochure."

10

Account — view, logout, delete

The account area is reached from the header profile icon. Visitors can view and edit their Basic Information, sign out, or delete the account entirely.

  1. Tap the profile icon in the header to open View Profile.
  2. Edit fields under Basic Information and save.
  3. Tap Logout → confirm in Logout Confirmation? → return to Login.
  4. Tap Delete Account for a destructive removal flow with explicit confirmation.
Product

Self-serve logout and delete are a baseline — universities don't want to be a support bottleneck for a prospect who changed their mind.

QA focus

Edits persist after refresh; logout clears the session and routes to Login; delete blocks login with the same credentials afterwards; confirmation modals require explicit action.

Marketing

"Students control their data — logout, delete, and report are one tap away."

Common questions

Ambassador Platform FAQs

The questions universities, prospects, support, and demo audiences ask most often about the platform.

What is The Ambassador Platform?
A public-facing web portal where prospective and incoming students discover real university ambassadors, read profiles, and chat one-to-one or in cohort groups. It's branded Student Ambassadors with Powered by Intoto in the header, and universities embed it on their own site.
How is this different from the Intoto mobile app?
The platform is a browser product focused on discovery and chat — meeting ambassadors before applying or enrolling. The mobile app is the full student lifecycle after enrolment: profile, travel, communities, events, and university admin. Different audience, different surface, different scope.
How do External Ambassadors appear on the platform?
Off-campus partners invited as External Ambassador show in the directory with a Staff badge (Figma Employee filter). Prospects filter by Staff, open the card, and tap Chat; the partner replies from the External Ambassador web portal. See Intoto External Ambassador Feature & Flow Guide for the fulfilment side.
Do visitors need to be enrolled students to use it?
No. The platform is built for prospective and incoming students — anyone can sign up with email and password, verify, and start chatting. Universities embed it to convert interest into conversations long before an application is in.
How does a visitor find an ambassador who matches them?
Two ways: search by name, program, or country in the search bar, or use the filter chips — Available Now, Students, Alumni, Staff, By Program, By Country. Active filters refresh the card grid; By Program and By Country open a picker first.
What's the difference between an Individual Chat and a Group Chat?
Individual chat is a one-to-one thread with a specific ambassador. Group chats are cohort or program rooms (e.g. International Students 2025) where many members and ambassadors talk together. Both live in the same Chat surface, behind two tabs.
What does the green Online dot mean?
The ambassador is currently available. It's a soft signal — even offline ambassadors usually respond within their stated response time — but Online suggests a real-time reply is likely.
Who moderates conversations?
The platform offers a per-chat report and delete via the three-dot menu, and the university's Intoto Admin and Super Admin handle moderation in the admin tools. Reports surface to those roles; visitors don't see moderation queues.
Can visitors send images in chat?
Yes — the composer has an attachment icon for image uploads, and group chats include a shared media gallery. Other file types are out of scope for this product surface.
What happens if a visitor doesn't verify their email?
The Verification Required! screen blocks the rest of the product until they click the link sent to their inbox. Login also redirects unverified accounts back to this gate, so chat and the directory require a verified address.
Can the platform be white-labelled?
In the current Figma scope, Powered by Intoto appears in the header alongside the university's Student Ambassadors branding. Full white-label is a product-roadmap conversation.
How do visitors delete their data?
From the profile icon → Delete Account. The flow confirms the destructive action, removes the account, and the email cannot log back in — a self-serve route designed with GDPR-style expectations in mind.
Why does the design specify three breakpoints?
International prospects browse on whatever they have — laptops at home, tablets at school, phones in transit. 1920, 1280, and 412 cover the realistic top, middle, and floor of that range without micro-optimising for every device.
