At a glance

Who the Student is

The Student is Intoto's primary end-user — an incoming or current university student preparing for campus life. They complete a profile and travel plan, discover events and communities, connect with classmates and travel buddies, and chat with ambassadors and staff. They are scoped to a single university and campus (assigned at invitation), and they do not manage other users, approve communities, or see admin statistics.

The one-paragraph version

A student logs in, is gently nudged to finish their profile and travel plan, then uses a content-focused home to discover events, ambassadors, and peers — registering for events, finding roommates and travel buddies, joining communities, and chatting — all configured per university by the backend.

End to end

The student journey

From the first login to active campus life, the path is built around one idea: remove friction before arrival, then open up engagement.

1

Sign in & set up

Log in with university credentials, pick a role if more than one, and complete a one-time profile setup with an optional photo.

2

Land on Home

A content-focused dashboard greets the student — with a completion alert if profile or travel data is still missing.

3

Complete onboarding

Guided, section-by-section completion of the Profile and the Travel Plan, with progress tracked and the alert clearing when done.

4

Discover & connect

Find classmates, roommates, and travel buddies; browse events and ambassadors; explore communities — all scoped to the campus.

5

Engage & stay connected

Register for events, join and create communities, and chat with peers, ambassadors, and staff — entirely in-platform.

Structure

How the app is organised

Four tabs are always available from the bottom bar. Everything a student does lives under one of them.

Tab 01
Home

The dashboard — completion alerts, banners, quick-action shortcuts, events, activities, and featured ambassadors.

Tab 02
Community

Browse, join, and post in university communities — and request to create new ones (subject to approval).

Tab 03
Chats

Direct and group messaging with classmates, ambassadors, and coordinators, with live unread counts.

Tab 04
Profile

Entry to My Profile sections, plus settings, FAQs, language, about, logout, and account deletion.

01

Getting in

Sign-in is via university credentials (Auth0). A first-time student may pass through a one-time profile setup before the main app, and multi-role accounts choose a role first. Students always land on the content dashboard — never the admin overview.

  1. Open the Intoto app and enter email/password, or use configured SSO.
  2. Wait for profile and role to load.
  3. Multiple roles → a Role Selection screen appears; choose Student.
  4. First-time user → complete a one-time Profile Setup (basic details + optional photo) → Continue.
  5. Land on Home; the four tabs appear — Home, Community, Chats, Profile.
Product

Profile setup is intentionally minimal — just enough to enter the app — with the deeper profile prompted later by dashboard alerts, so first impression isn't a long form.

QA focus

Single-role students land on the student dashboard (not admin overview); multi-role shows Role Selection; first-time setup gates the tab bar; an incomplete account shows a completion alert on Home.

Marketing

"Sign in once with your university account and you're in — your whole campus, ready before you arrive."

02

Home dashboard

The student home is content- and action-oriented — not admin stat cards. It opens with a completion alert when onboarding is unfinished, then surfaces banners, quick-action shortcuts, events, activities, and featured ambassadors. Every section is configured by the backend, in API order.

  1. Land on Home after login; the university logo and name sit in the header.
  2. If profile or travel data is incomplete, read the completion alert at the top — with a progress ring for profile.
  3. Tap the alert to jump straight to My Profile or the Travel Plan hub.
  4. Swipe banners, tap quick actions, browse upcoming events and ambassadors.
  5. Pull down to refresh counts, events, and alerts from the server.

Product

Students get a content dashboard, not admin metrics, by design — the alert turns onboarding into a guided nudge rather than a wall of forms, and backend ordering keeps each university's home relevant.

QA focus

No admin Quick Way stat cards render; sections appear only when the API sends them and in API order; the alert routes to the right hub and clears when complete; pull-to-refresh updates.

Marketing

"Your campus home — events, people, and what to do next, all in one feed that knows exactly where you left off."

03

My Profile

My Profile is the student's own data hub: a list of sections with completion status, driven by the studentForm config. A guided Save → Next flow walks through sections in order, and finishing them clears the dashboard alert and makes the student visible to peers and coordinators. A Shared by University section here holds documents the university has sent the student — one-to-one or as part of a group — to view and download.

  1. Open from the profile alert, the Profile tab → My Profile, or a related flow.
  2. Tap a section row (each shows complete/incomplete status).
  3. Fill the API-configured fields, then tap Save.
  4. Tap Next to move through sections in order (or Previous to go back).
  5. Leaving with unsaved edits prompts to Save or Discard.
Typical sectionWhat it captures
Basic Details · PictureIdentity, photo for discovery lists
PreferencesInterests & social prefs — powers peer matching
Contact & EmergencyPhone, addresses, up to 3 emergency contacts

Product

Sections and order come from university config, not hardcoding — so the same hub serves every institution. Completion is the unlock for visibility and matching, which is why it's nudged so prominently.

QA focus

Sections load with status from the API; Save persists and updates status; Next/Previous follow API order; unsaved-changes prompt fires; photo view/change/remove works; document upload shows review status.

Marketing

"A profile that builds itself one step at a time — finish it and your university and future classmates can find you."

04

Travel Plan

The Travel Plan captures how and when the student arrives — transport, documents, and a signed consent — so coordinators can support arrival. It runs on the travelForm config, and completing it unlocks connecting with other travellers.

  1. Open from the Travel Plan quick action or the travel completion alert.
  2. Fill Travel Details — arrival date/time, transport mode, flight/train, destination → Save.
  3. Upload required Travel Documents (tickets, visa, accommodation proofs).
  4. In Signature Documents, check the consent box, then draw or upload a signature → Submit.
  5. When complete, tap Connect with Other Travellers to open the travel-buddy list.
Product

Consent is gated before submission so legal acknowledgement is never skipped, and the social reward (travel buddies) is deliberately placed after completion to motivate finishing the plan.

QA focus

Signature submit is blocked until consent is checked; sections save and mark complete; the travel hub's Connect button is blocked until the plan is complete (shows a message); Program Details is edited from My Profile, not here.

Marketing

"Tell us how you're arriving once — and meet students travelling the same way before you land."

05

Social discovery

Three discovery lists help students build a social graph before arrival — classmates, roommates, and travel buddies. All share the same search-and-filter pattern and lead to a visibility-controlled peer profile with a path to chat.

  1. Open Connect with Classmates, Find Roommate, or Travel Buddy from Home.
  2. Search (3+ characters, debounced) or tap Filter.
  3. Pick a category in the sidebar, select options (multi-select), then Apply — or use a date range for Visit Schedule.
  4. Tap a row to open the peer profile (only the sections the peer chose to share).
  5. Start a Chat or contact action where available.
Product

One shared list pattern across three intents keeps the experience learnable, and peer-profile visibility rules protect students while still enabling connection.

QA focus

Search debounces at 3+ chars; filter apply/cancel/clear-all work; multi-select and date filters behave; peer profiles show only allowed sections and no admin actions; chat opens from a profile.

Marketing

"Find your roommate, your classmates, and your travel crew before day one — friendships start before you arrive."

06

Events

Students discover university events from Home, register in a tap (answering any extra questions), see their registrations under My Events, and may be prompted for feedback after attending.

  1. Find events via Upcoming Events on Home, the Events quick action, or View All.
  2. Open an event → read title, date, location, description, capacity.
  3. Tap Register Now; if the event has questions, answer them and submit.
  4. See a success confirmation; the event appears under My Events on Home.
  5. After attending, a feedback popup may appear — Give Feedback or No Thanks.
Product

Registration and feedback live in the same app as onboarding, so engagement is continuous — and custom questions let organisers collect what they need at the point of intent.

QA focus

Register succeeds and reflects on My Events; required questions block submit until answered; success confirms with event name and date; the feedback popup's Give Feedback and No Thanks both work without crashing.

Marketing

"From orientation to club nights — discover what's on and reserve your spot in seconds."

07

Community

Students browse and join communities organised by interest, program, or culture, read and create posts as members, and can request to create new communities — which go through admin approval rather than going live instantly.

  1. Open the Community tab and explore feed, my communities, or discover.
  2. Tap a community → profile, members, and posts; Join if not a member.
  3. In a joined community, read the feed, open a post, or Create post.
  4. To start one: Create Community → name, description, images → Submit.
  5. See the approval message — the community goes live only after admin approval.
Product

Student-created communities are gated for safety: same create flow as admins, but the outcome is "submitted for approval" rather than instant publish — moderation without blocking participation.

QA focus

Join updates membership; member-only post creation is enforced; create shows "submitted for approval" (not instant live) and the community is not public until approved.

Marketing

"Find your people — join communities by interest, program, or home country, or start your own."

08

Chat & account

Rounding out the experience: in-platform messaging with peers, ambassadors, and staff; notifications and pending role invitations; and the Profile tab for settings, language, support, and account control.

  1. Chat: open the Chats tab, pick a thread, read history, and send — with live unread counts.
  2. Message someone new from their profile in any discovery or ambassador list via Chat.
  3. Notifications: tap the bell on Home for approvals, reminders, and chat alerts.
  4. Invitations: accept an additional role from the alert, or later via the envelope icon.
  5. Account: Profile tab holds My Profile, FAQs, Settings, language, About, logout, and delete account.
Product

Messaging is peer-to-peer and support-oriented — students reach ambassadors and classmates directly from the profiles they discover, keeping every interaction inside the platform.

QA focus

Threads load and send works with live unread counts; chat starts from a profile; bell opens notifications and deep-links where supported; envelope shows pending invites; logout clears the session.

Marketing

"Real help from real people — message ambassadors and classmates the moment you need them, all in one app."

Common questions

Student FAQs

The questions students, support, and demo audiences ask most often about the role.

What can I do as a student in Intoto?
Onboard, complete your travel and arrival details, discover your campus, join communities, register for events, and message ambassadors — all from one app.
How do I get started?
Sign in with the credentials from your invitation, then work through onboarding and your profile. Forms save as you go, so you can stop and resume without losing progress.
What travel and arrival information do I provide?
The travel and arrival forms collect your trip and document details, and where required you add a signature. This is shared with the university to help coordinate your arrival.
Why does completing my profile matter?
A complete profile unlocks the full experience and ensures the people helping you — ambassadors and staff — have what they need. The app nudges you to finish any incomplete sections.
How do I find and message an ambassador?
On your Home dashboard, scroll to the Ambassadors carousel, tap a mentor’s card to open their profile, then tap Chat. The conversation opens in your Chats tab.
How do I join a community?
Open the Community tab, browse the available communities, and tap Join. You can then read the feed, open posts, and post yourself.
How do I register for an event?
Find it on the Upcoming Events carousel or the events list, open it, and tap Register while the registration window is open. It then appears under your events.
Is my personal data safe?
Yes — sign-in is via Auth0, your data is stored encrypted on device, and uploaded documents are checked for tampering.
Where do I get help?
Use the Support option for official help, or message an ambassador for friendly, peer guidance on day-to-day questions.
