At a glance

Who the Ambassador is

An Ambassador is a university peer mentor — a current student, alumnus, or external partner — who helps incoming and current students navigate campus life. They answer questions, chat with students, and maintain a public ambassador profile that appears on the student Home dashboard so newcomers know whom to contact. They work within one university and are not operational administrators: no inviting staff, assigning students, or approving communities.

The one-paragraph version

An ambassador logs in, completes their public mentor profile (the Ambassador Info section), and spends most of their time in Chats answering students who found them on the dashboard — keeping their photo and details current so they stay visible and trusted on the student Ambassadors carousel.

A day in the role

How the work flows

The ambassador's loop is simple: stay visible with a complete profile, then be responsive to the students who reach out.

1

Accept & sign in

Accept the ambassador invitation, choose the Ambassador role if the account is multi-role, and land on the ambassador home.

2

Complete the mentor profile

Add a photo and fill Ambassador Info — bio, programs, languages, availability — so the profile is trusted and visible.

3

Respond to students

Work the Chats inbox — answer questions about arrival, campus, events, and point students to official channels when needed.

4

Stay engaged

Browse and register for events, participate in communities, and keep programs and languages up to date as intakes change.

5

Switch context if dual-role

Student ambassadors can switch to the Student role to manage their own onboarding, then back to mentor.

01

Getting in

Ambassadors sign in with university credentials and accept an ambassador invitation. Many ambassadors are also Students on the same account, so role selection is common — and External Ambassadors confirm a campus and ambassador type when accepting.

  1. Open the app and sign in with email/password or SSO.
  2. If invited, the alert offers Accept or Do it later (deferred invites wait under the envelope icon).
  3. On accept, select campus and, for External Ambassadors, an ambassador type if prompted.
  4. Multi-role accounts choose Ambassador at Role Selection (or switch later).
  5. Complete profile setup / Ambassador Info when nudged, then land on Home.
Product

The dual Student+Ambassador account is a first-class case — role selection keeps the two contexts cleanly separate rather than merging mentor and personal flows.

QA focus

Invite Accept/Do-it-later works; deferred invites appear under the envelope; External Ambassador prompts for campus + ambassador type; switching to Ambassador loads the ambassador dashboard (no student-only alerts).

Marketing

"Become the friendly face new students look for — set up your mentor profile in minutes."

02

Home dashboard

The ambassador home is outreach-focused, not governance. It shows a completion alert when the profile is unfinished, plus a Quick Way oriented around messages and students, quick actions, events, and banners — all in backend-defined order.

  1. Land on Home; the university logo and name sit in the header (tap for read-only context).
  2. If your profile or Ambassador Info is incomplete, read and tap the completion alert → My Profile.
  3. Scan outreach-focused Quick Way cards — e.g. Total Messages, Total Students, Total Events.
  4. Use quick actions (Events, Support, My Profile) and browse upcoming events.
  5. Tap the bell for notifications; pull to refresh after heavy chat activity.
Product

The backend sends only outreach-relevant sections — no governance stats, pending queues, or unassigned students — so the home reinforces the mentor role rather than overwhelming it.

QA focus

Admin governance cards (Manage Users, pending approvals, unassigned students) do not appear; the completion alert routes to My Profile / Ambassador Info and clears when complete; the header collapses gracefully when the logo is missing.

Marketing

"A home built for helping — see who needs you and what's happening on campus at a glance."

03

The ambassador profile

Unlike admin roles, ambassadors maintain a section-based profile (ambassadorForm) — and the mandatory Ambassador Info section is exactly what students see on mentor cards: bio, programs, languages, availability, interests, and ambassador type.

  1. Open My Profile from the Profile tab, the completion alert, or a quick action.
  2. Tap the profile photo to change it (pick → crop → upload) — friendly photos rank higher on the carousel.
  3. Tap Ambassador Info and fill bio, programs/faculties, languages, availability, and interests.
  4. Use lookups (tap → search → select) for type, programs, and multi-select chips for languages/interests.
  5. Tap Save; the completion ring on Home updates on refresh once the backend marks it complete.
Product

The profile is the product surface students judge — so it's a rich, editable section hub, and completeness directly governs carousel visibility and ranking.

QA focus

Ambassador Info saves and marks complete; lookups and multi-selects persist; photo change updates the header; required-field validation blocks save; incomplete profiles are hidden or ranked lower on the student carousel.

Marketing

"Show students who you are — your languages, your program, and how you can help — in a profile they can trust."

04

Chats

Chats is where ambassadors do their real work. Students who tap an ambassador on the Home carousel start a conversation here, and the ambassador answers questions about arrival, housing, registration, events, and campus life.

  1. Open the Chats tab to see the inbox of recent threads with unread badges.
  2. Tap a thread to open the conversation, read history, and reply.
  3. Attach an image or file where enabled; use search to find a student by name.
  4. Point students to Support, Events, or a Community in your reply when a question is outside your scope.
  5. Leave threads open for follow-up; new messages and counts update in real time.
Product

Messaging is one-to-one and support-oriented by design — ambassadors aren't broadcast channels, so there's no bulk-messaging surface in this role.

QA focus

Threads load and send works; unread counts update live; chat opens from a student/ambassador profile; no bulk-message tools are exposed; empty inbox shows guidance to complete the profile.

Marketing

"Real answers from someone who's been there — students get human help from day one."

05

Students & discovery

When configured, ambassadors can browse the students they're allowed to view and open a visibility-controlled profile — with Chat as the primary action. This is support, not administration: no assign, suspend, or coordinator tools.

  1. Open Total Students from Quick Way (when shown).
  2. Search (3+ characters, debounced) or filter by campus, program, or intake.
  3. Tap a student row to open their profile (only sections allowed by visibility rules).
  4. Tap Chat to start or continue a conversation.
  5. Direct the student to official channels or Support when a question exceeds your scope.
Product

Discovery exists to enable outreach, not oversight — the only meaningful action from a student profile is to start a conversation.

QA focus

Search debounces at 3+ chars; filters apply/clear; student profiles show only allowed sections; no admin actions (assign/suspend) appear; Chat opens from the profile.

Marketing

"Reach out first — find the students who need a hand and start the conversation."

06

Events & community

Ambassadors participate in campus life like engaged students — browsing and registering for events, and joining and posting in communities — but they do not approve events or pending communities.

  1. Find events via the Upcoming Events carousel, the Events quick action, or Total Events.
  2. Open an event and Register while the window is open; registered events show under My Events.
  3. Open the Community tab, explore communities, and Join ones you want to support.
  4. Read feeds, open posts, and Create post to share welcome content or answer questions.
  5. Escalate anything beyond your scope to Support or staff rather than acting on it.
Product

Ambassadors model good participation — present and helpful in events and communities — without governance powers, keeping moderation with the coordinator roles.

QA focus

Register works and reflects on My Events; community join and member posting work; no approve/reject controls for events or pending communities appear for this role.

Marketing

"Be where students are — at events and in communities, helping the campus feel like home."

07

How students find you

Understanding the student-facing path is why profile completeness matters so much. A student discovers an ambassador entirely through the Home carousel and the public profile.

  1. A student logs in and lands on their Home dashboard.
  2. They scroll to the Ambassadors carousel and see your photo, name, and short descriptor.
  3. They tap your card to open your public ambassador profile.
  4. They tap Chat to start a conversation with you.
  5. Incomplete Ambassador Info or a missing photo lowers trust and visibility on that carousel.
Product

The carousel is the entire top-of-funnel for ambassador contact, which is why the app nudges profile completion so persistently.

QA focus

Complete profiles appear on the student Ambassadors carousel; the public profile renders photo, name, and descriptor; the student Chat CTA opens a thread to the right ambassador.

Marketing

"Featured on every new student's home screen — a complete profile is your invitation to connect."

Common questions

Ambassador FAQs

The questions ambassadors, support, and demo audiences ask most often about the role.

What’s the difference between an Ambassador and an External Ambassador?
An Ambassador is usually a current student or alumnus (often also holding the Student role); an External Ambassador is an off-campus partner invited with the External ambassador type. Both use the same flows and profile form.
Can I be both a Student and an Ambassador?
Yes — that’s common. You choose which context to work in at login or via the role switcher, and the app keeps the two cleanly separate.
How do I switch between my Student and Ambassador roles?
Use the role switcher on Home or Profile, or pick the role at login. Home and the tabs reload for whichever role you choose.
Why is completing my profile so important?
Your Ambassador Info and photo are exactly what students see on the Ambassadors carousel. Incomplete profiles are hidden or ranked lower, so completeness is how students find and trust you.
What is the Ambassador Info section?
The public, mentor-facing part of your profile: bio, type, programs, languages, availability, and interests. Everything students see on your card comes from here.
Where do students reach me?
In Chats. A student taps your card on their home carousel, opens your profile, and taps Chat — the conversation lands in your Chats inbox.
Can I message lots of students at once?
No. Ambassador messaging is one-to-one and support-oriented; mass communication to the student body is a staff capability.
Can I approve events or communities?
No. You participate — register for events, join and post in communities — but approvals belong to the Event and Community Coordinator roles.
What should I do with a question I can’t answer?
Point the student to the right place — Support, an event, or a community — especially for official matters like visas, admissions, or registration holds.
