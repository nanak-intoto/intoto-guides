At a glance

Who the Mobility Program Admin is

The Mobility Program Admin is Mobility Office staff responsible for day-to-day academic mobility operations. They review student applications, make approval decisions, nominate accepted students to partner universities, maintain programs and partner information, and generate operational reports.

The one-paragraph version

A Mobility Program Admin starts in the Command Center, checks pending approvals, reviews applications in detail, approves or rejects students, nominates approved students to partner universities, then uses program, partner, document, report, and settings screens to keep the exchange operation current.

A day in the role

How the work flows

The admin workflow follows the student mobility lifecycle from application review through nomination and operational reporting.

1

Open Command Center

Check total students, pending approvals, active programs, partner count, recent approval activity, and quick actions.

2

Review pending applications

Open the Approval Queue, filter the list, inspect application detail, and verify student profile, GPA, documents, and notes.

3

Decide on the application

Approve, reject, or request more info. The decision determines whether the student becomes eligible for nomination.

4

Nominate approved students

Use the Nomination Hub to select eligible students and assign them to partner universities.

5

Maintain operations

Review programs, partner universities, documents, reports, notifications, and settings so the office stays current.

01

Command Center

The first screen for daily mobility operations.

  1. Show the admin name and title in the header.
  2. Present a 2x2 stat grid: total students, pending approvals, active programs, total partners.
  3. Show recent approval activity: approved, nominated, rejected, reviewed.
  4. Surface quick actions and a pending approvals summary with one-tap access to the queue.
Product

Command Center keeps operational attention on the next decision, especially pending approvals.

QA focus

Stats match source lists, quick actions route correctly, and pending approvals open the Approval Queue.

Marketing

"Your international office starts each day with approvals, programs, partners, and student movement in one view."

02

Student management

Search, filter, and inspect mobility students before making decisions.

  1. Open the student directory with all mobility students.
  2. Search by name and filter by status: All, Approved, Pending, Rejected.
  3. Tap a student card to open full profile and mobility information.
  4. Review GPA, year, major, program, destination, and current stage.
  5. Use action buttons to approve, request more info, or reject where the workflow allows.
Product

Student management gives the admin a single operational directory instead of scattered spreadsheets.

QA focus

Search, filters, status badges, card taps, and student detail actions must stay in sync.

Marketing

"Find every mobility student, understand their stage, and act without leaving the app."

03

Approval Queue

The decision queue for submitted mobility applications.

  1. Open a filterable queue of applications.
  2. Highlight high-GPA applications with a priority badge when GPA is 3.8 or higher.
  3. Tap an application to open Application Detail.
  4. Review student info, documents, review notes, and eligibility context.
  5. Choose approve, reject, or request more info.
DecisionMeaning
ApproveStudent becomes eligible for nomination to a partner university.
RejectApplication is declined and should no longer proceed to nomination.
Request more infoApplication remains unresolved until the missing detail is supplied.
Product

The queue makes Review an explicit lifecycle stage owned by the admin.

QA focus

Priority badge threshold, filters, decision buttons, and post-decision status changes are key test points.

Marketing

"Turn application review into a clear queue with traceable decisions."

04

Nomination Hub

Where approved students are assigned to partner universities.

  1. Open Nomination Hub after applications are approved.
  2. Review nominated versus pending counts.
  3. See eligible approved students in the list.
  4. Select a partner university from the dropdown.
  5. Use the nominate toggle next to the student to assign the nomination.
Product

Nomination is the handoff from internal approval to partner university acceptance.

QA focus

Only approved students should be eligible; partner selection must be required before nomination.

Marketing

"Move approved students from decision to destination with a controlled nomination flow."

05

Programs

The catalog of mobility opportunities the office manages.

  1. Open the program catalog.
  2. Review cards for type, duration, deadline, enrollment, and status.
  3. Tap a program for detail: description, funding, partner universities, and stats.
  4. Use the program view to confirm what is available before advising or nominating students.

06

Partner universities

The directory of institutions that receive mobility nominations.

  1. Open the partner university directory.
  2. Search partner universities and review country, city, student count, agreement status, and QS ranking.
  3. Tap a partner card for stats, description, website, agreement status, and active programs.
  4. Use partner detail to choose the right destination during nomination.

QA focus

Marketing

"Give the mobility office a live map of partner universities and active agreements."

07

Documents

A document view for admin review and status visibility.

  1. Open the admin document view.
  2. Review summary stats: total, approved, pending.
  3. Filter documents by category.
  4. Use document state to support application decisions and follow-up requests.
Product

Document status keeps application review grounded in required evidence.

QA focus

Category filters and approval/pending counts should agree with the document list.

Marketing

"Know which documents are ready, pending, or blocking the next decision."

Operational reporting for the mobility office.

  4. Use download or export actions where available.
  5. For draft reports, use Finalize before distributing the report.
  6. Generate a report when operational reporting needs a fresh output.

Common questions

Mobility Program Admin FAQs

What is the Mobility Program Admin role?
It is the Mobility Office role that manages day-to-day mobility operations: reviewing applications, approving or rejecting them, nominating students to partner universities, maintaining programs and partners, reviewing documents, and generating reports.
Is Mobility Program a paid feature?
Yes. Mobility Program is a standalone paid module in the Paid Features catalog. When it is Active, the Mobility Program Admin role and related approval, nomination, program, partner, document, and reporting screens are available.
What is the difference between Mobility Program Admin and University Super Admin?
The Mobility Program Admin runs operational mobility workflows at the student, application, program, and partner level. The University Super Admin governs the broader university tenant, users, configuration, and entitlement visibility. They can be assigned separately depending on how the university staffs its teams.
What application decisions can this admin make?
The brief defines three actions: approve, reject, and request more info. Approved students become eligible for nomination; rejected applications stop; request-more-info keeps the application unresolved until missing details are provided.
How does nomination work?
After approval, the admin opens Nomination Hub, reviews eligible students, chooses a partner university, and nominates the student. This moves the process from internal review toward partner university acceptance.
What reports are included?
The brief lists Annual, Quarterly, Evaluation, Survey, and Financial reports. Each report can have a detail page with summary, key metrics, and download or export actions.
Does this update change the Student guide?
No. This guide only documents the Mobility Program Admin role. Student-facing mobility details are intentionally not added to the Student guide in this update.
