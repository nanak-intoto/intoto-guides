At a glance

Who the Sub Coordinator is

The Third Party Sub Coordinator is the field operator on a partner organisation's team — the greeter, driver, or campus host who actually meets arriving students. A Third Party Coordinator assigns them a batch of students with a service window and a work brief; the sub-coordinator works that list one student at a time, marking how each arrival went. They see only the student details the coordinator chose to share, and they don't assign students, manage other users, or touch events and communities.

The one-paragraph version

A sub-coordinator opens their assigned students, reads each Arrival Request Details (brief, dates, and the fields shared with them), then marks status — Attended, Completed, or Not Attended with a reason and notes — and can request unassign or request reassign when they can't take a student.

01

Getting in

Sign-in is via the partner credentials issued for the university. Multi-role accounts choose Third Party Sub Coordinator, and Home reloads to the assigned-students work list — not a coordinator's pool or an admin surface.

  1. Open the app and sign in with email/password or SSO.
  2. Wait for the profile and role to load.
  3. Single role → land on the assigned-students list; multi-role → choose the role at Role Selection.
  4. A pending invitation shows an alert at login — Accept or I'll do it later (envelope icon later).
  5. The four tabs appear — Home, Community, Chats, Profile.
Product

The role opens straight to the work list because the sub-coordinator's job is operational and time-sensitive — no dashboards to wade through before the first pickup.

QA focus

Single-role lands on the assigned-students list; multi-role shows Role Selection; switching reloads the work list (no assign or admin tools appear).

Marketing

"Open the app and you're working — your students, today, right there."

02

My assigned students

The core screen is the list of students currently assigned to this sub-coordinator — searchable, paginated, and filterable — each row showing the student and their current arrival status.

  1. Open the assigned students list on Home.
  2. Search by name and filter (e.g. by date or status) to find the right student.
  3. Read each row's status chip — Assigned, Attended, or Completed.
  4. Tap a row to open that student's Arrival Request Details.
  5. Pull to refresh to pick up newly assigned students or coordinator changes.
Product

Showing only the students assigned to this person keeps the work list focused and accountable — they own exactly this set, no more.

QA focus

The list shows only students assigned to the signed-in sub-coordinator; search and filters re-query; status chips match the latest update; refresh reflects new assignments and removals.

Marketing

"Everyone you're meeting today, in one clear list — no clutter, no guesswork."

03

Arrival request details

Tapping a student opens Arrival Request Details — the brief for that pickup. It shows the assignment status, who assigned it, the service date range, the work description, and the student fields the coordinator chose to share.

  1. Open a student from the list to see Arrival Request Details.
  2. Read the Assignment Status and the assignment date range.
  3. Read the work description — what the coordinator needs you to do.
  4. Use the shared contact and arrival fields (only those enabled for you) to make the pickup.
  5. See who assigned the student so you know who to coordinate with.
Product

One brief per student — status, dates, instructions, and just the necessary details — gives the field operator everything to act and nothing to wade through.

QA focus

Only fields the coordinator enabled are visible; hidden fields don't render; status, dates, work description, and assigned-by all populate; the screen is read-only except for the status action.

Marketing

"Everything you need for the pickup, and only what you need — privacy-respecting by design."

04

Marking arrival status

The one write action of the role: recording how the arrival went. From the student (or a multi-select on the list) the sub-coordinator sets the status, which flows straight back to the coordinator's tracking.

  1. Open a student (or select several) and tap update status / Take Action.
  2. Choose Attended when the student was met and the pickup is underway.
  3. Choose Completed when the handover is done.
  4. Choose Not Attended when the pickup didn't happen — this prompts for a reason (next section).
  5. Submit — the new status appears on the student and updates the coordinator's counts.
Product

A clear three-state outcome (attended, completed, not attended) keeps reporting honest and lets the coordinator step in the moment a pickup goes sideways.

QA focus

A status must be selected before submit; Attended and Completed need no reason; Not Attended requires one; the update applies to all selected students and the new status renders without a refresh.

Marketing

"Two taps to confirm a student is safely met — your coordinator sees it instantly."

05

Reasons & notes

When a pickup can't be completed, Not Attended captures why. The sub-coordinator picks from a fixed set of reasons and, for the open-ended case, adds a note — so the coordinator understands exactly what happened.

  1. Choose Not Attended on the status action.
  2. Pick a reason: Student not answering phone, Flight delayed, Student arrived late, Incorrect arrival details, or Other.
  3. If you pick Other, add a note explaining what happened (required for Other).
  4. Add optional notes for any reason to give the coordinator context.
  5. Submit — the reason and notes attach to the student's arrival record.
Product

A short, structured reason list keeps failure data consistent and analysable, while the notes field captures the human detail that doesn't fit a category.

QA focus

The five reasons render; selecting Other makes the note mandatory and blocks submit when empty; notes are optional for other reasons; the chosen reason and notes appear on the coordinator's Arrival Request Details.

Marketing

"When a plan slips, say exactly why in a tap — so the right person can step in fast."

06

Unassign & reassign requests

A sub-coordinator can't always take a student they've been given — a clash, a shift change, the wrong region. Rather than dropping the ball, they raise a request for the coordinator to act on, keeping the student covered.

  1. On a student you can't take, choose Unassign Request or Reassign Request from the status action.
  2. The request is sent to the Third Party Coordinator who assigned the student.
  3. The coordinator reviews it in Notifications and Accepts or declines.
  4. On accept, the student is removed from your list (unassign) or moved to another sub-coordinator (reassign).
  5. Refresh — your list reflects the coordinator's decision.
Product

Requests keep authority with the coordinator while giving the field operator a clean way to hand back work — no student silently falls through the cracks.

QA focus

Requests reach the assigning coordinator's Notifications; the student stays on your list until the request is accepted; accept removes or reassigns; decline leaves the student with you.

Marketing

"Can't cover a pickup? Hand it back in a tap — your coordinator takes it from there."

07

Chat & account

Messaging keeps the field operator in sync with the coordinator and students, and notifications surface new assignments and decisions. The Profile tab holds account settings; community access is browse-only.

  1. Open the Chats tab to message the coordinator and students about arrival times and meeting points.
  2. Tap the bell for notifications — new students assigned to you, request decisions, arrivals due soon.
  3. Use the envelope for deferred role invitations.
  4. Browse the Community tab where permitted (no moderation powers here).
  5. Open Profile for account info, FAQs, language, and logout; use Support to escalate.
Product

Notifications are tuned to the field — new assignments and request decisions — so the sub-coordinator always knows what's landed on their list.

QA focus

Threads load and send; notifications deep-link to the assigned student; no assign or admin controls appear; logout clears the session.

Marketing

"A direct line to your coordinator and your students — and a ping the moment work lands."

Common questions

Third Party Sub Coordinator FAQs

The questions field teams, support, and demo audiences ask most often about the role.

What is the Sub Coordinator responsible for?
Working the students a coordinator assigns to you — reading each arrival brief, meeting the student, and marking how it went (attended, completed, or not attended with a reason).
How do students end up on my list?
A Third Party Coordinator assigns you a batch with a service date range and a work brief; they appear on your assigned-students list and you’re notified.
Why can’t I see all of a student’s details?
You see only the fields the coordinator chose to share for that assignment — deliberate data-minimisation, so hidden fields simply don’t appear.
What statuses can I set?
Attended (met, pickup underway), Completed (handover done), or Not Attended (didn’t happen, needs a reason). The coordinator sees your status live.
What reasons can I give for Not Attended?
Student not answering phone, Flight delayed, Student arrived late, Incorrect arrival details, or Other. Choosing Other requires a note; notes are optional otherwise.
Can I mark several students at once?
Yes, where multi-select is provided — handy for a group that arrived together. A status must be chosen before you submit.
I can’t take a student I was assigned — what do I do?
Raise an Unassign or Reassign Request; it goes to the coordinator, who Accepts or declines. The student stays on your list until they act.
Can I reassign a student myself?
No — only the coordinator can reassign. You can request it, but the move and choosing who it goes to stays with them.
Does my status update reach the coordinator immediately?
Yes — it flows into their Arrival Request Details and dashboard counts, so they always see the current picture.
