At a glance

Who the Intoto Admin is

The Intoto Admin is the platform-level administrator for Intoto — they operate across every university (tenant) on the platform, not within a single institution like a University Super Admin. They onboard and manage universities, invite top-level university administrators, monitor platform-wide health, and govern users, communities, events, and reports at a cross-tenant level. A university context switcher lets them drill into one tenant and step back out to the platform-wide view at any time.

The one-paragraph version

An Intoto Admin logs into a platform control panel branded for Intoto, reads cross-tenant health from Quick Way stat cards, then onboards and manages universities, provisions paid feature modules for each tenant, invites and controls users across all tenants, invites University Super Admins, browses global directories of students, staff, communities and events, and triages reports platform-wide — switching university context whenever a single tenant needs a closer look.

A day in the role

How the work flows

The platform admin's day is a loop: read the health of the whole platform, onboard and govern the tenants and people that need attention, then drill into any one university when a case demands it.

1

Sign in to the platform panel

Log in with Intoto platform credentials. The dashboard loads with Intoto branding and a platform-wide, cross-tenant view — not a single university.

2

Read platform health

Quick Way stat cards show live counts for each group — students, ambassadors, the coordinator roles, sub-coordinators, and university super admins. Click any card to open its own dashboard.

3

Onboard & govern tenants

Add a new university, edit branding, suspend or re-activate institutions, and invite each university's first Super Admin.

4

Manage people across tenants

Invite, resend, withdraw, suspend, or re-initiate accounts for any role at any university — all from one cross-tenant list.

5

Focus on one university

Switch university context from the header or filters to scope lists and counts to a single tenant, then clear it to return platform-wide.

6

Oversee & communicate

Triage reports, review communities and events across tenants, and reach users directly through chat, bulk email, and notifications.

Product catalog

Features Intoto provides to universities

Intoto is sold as seven standalone paid modules — not an all-or-nothing license. The Intoto Admin provisions which modules each tenant receives, with contract dates and (for User Management) seat capacity. The University Super Admin sees the result read-only on My University → Features. Full detail for every module is in the Paid Features product catalog.

  1. During Add University or Edit University, configure which of the seven modules are enabled for the tenant.
  2. Set Active state, Start Date, and End Date per module per the commercial agreement.
  3. For User Management, set Total Capacity and optional domestic vs international seat limits.
  4. Save — the tenant's web portal and mobile app expose only the provisioned modules.
  5. To add a module mid-contract, update the university and enable the new module; it appears on the university's Features tab with its own dates.

01

Getting in

Sign-in is via Intoto platform credentials (Auth0 or configured SSO). The defining detail for this role: the app loads a cross-tenant, platform-wide view rather than a single university. Role selection only appears when one person also holds another role.

  1. Open the Intoto app and enter email/password, or use the configured SSO method.
  2. Wait for the app to load the profile and role.
  3. Single role (Intoto Admin only) → land directly on the Dashboard (the platform analytics home).
  4. Multiple roles → a Role Selection screen appears first; choose Intoto Admin.
  5. Pending invitations → an alert offers Accept or Do it later; deferred ones wait under the notifications area.
Product

Loading platform-wide is deliberate — the admin operates above any single institution. Role selection is conditional, shown only to multi-role accounts, so single-role admins skip a step.

QA focus

Single-role lands on the platform dashboard (Intoto branding, cross-tenant cards), not a university home. Multi-role shows Role Selection; switching to Intoto Admin reloads platform-admin sections and global filters.

Marketing

"One secure sign-in opens the entire platform — every university, every user, from a single command center."

02

The dashboard — Quick Way & analytics

Dashboard is the admin's home screen. A Dashboard Overview band of Quick Way stat cards sits up top, a set of analytics charts fills the rest, and a right-hand rail carries a university filter (“All”), a From / To date range, and an export control. Every Quick Way card is a doorway — clicking one opens its own dedicated dashboard for that group, with its own list, stats, and actions.

  1. Open Dashboard from the sidebar (the default screen after login).
  2. Read the top bar — date and greeting, language switcher, the notifications bell, and your profile menu.
  3. Set context in the right rail: pick a university from the “All” dropdown, choose a From / To date range, and use export to download.
  4. Scan the Quick Way stat cards under Dashboard Overview for the live count of each group.
  5. Click any Quick Way card to open its own dashboard — a focused screen for that group.
  6. Read the analytics widgets below and in the rail; each has its own export button.

03

University management — tenants

This is the core Intoto Admin capability that no other role has: creating, viewing, editing, and controlling the lifecycle of universities on the platform. University Super Admins cannot do this — only the platform admin can onboard a tenant, edit its branding, and suspend or re-activate it.

  1. Open from the Manage Universities quick action, the Total Universities stat card, or the app-bar university picker.
  2. On University List, search by name and narrow with status chips — All · Active · Pending · Suspended.
  3. Tap a row to open University Details — logo, status, campus summary, key metrics, configuration, and actions.
  4. To onboard, tap Add University: enter name, optional logo, identifier/domain, primary contact, an initial campus, and optionally invite the first Super Admin → Submit.
  5. To control a tenant, open its details and tap Suspend (or Activate) and confirm; the status badge updates.
  6. Configure paid feature modules for the tenant — see Features for universities and the Paid Features catalog.

04

Manage Users — across all tenants

Manage Users is the cross-tenant account-control hub: everyone invited to or active on the platform, at any university, filterable by status, with actions that depend on where each person sits in the invitation lifecycle. The defining difference from a University Super Admin is the University dimension on every list and filter.

  1. Open from the Manage User quick action, the Total Invites stat, or a directory action.
  2. Tap a status chip — All, Invited, Accepted, Withdrawn, Rejected, Suspended.
  3. Search by name or email, or tap Filter → choose University, Campus, and/or Role → Apply.
  4. Tap one person's row to see the actions available for their status.
  5. Or enter selection mode, select many rows, and tap Take Action for bulk email.

Product

Actions are state-aware by design — the admin only ever sees moves valid for that account — and the University filter is what turns a single-tenant tool into a platform-wide one.

QA focus

Each status chip filters correctly; the right action set shows per status; the filter sidebar includes University (unlike the Super Admin); bulk selection → Take Action → email works across tenants.

Marketing

"Full control of every account on the platform — invite, resend, suspend, or restore — across every university, in one list."

05

Add User — invite anyone, anywhere

A single form sends platform invitations by email for any invitable role — including University Super Admin. The fields adapt to the role, and one rule stands out for this role: a University is required for every university-scoped invite, while a campus is required for operational roles but skipped for a Super Admin.

  1. Open Add User from the dashboard quick action.
  2. Select the role (Student, Coordinator, Ambassador, University Super Admin, etc.).
  3. Select the University — required for all university-scoped roles and for University Super Admin.
  4. Enter one email, or import a file for bulk invites.
  5. Select campus — required for most operational roles; skipped for University Super Admin.
  6. Set role-specific fields (Ambassador Type, profile visibility, invite type), pick an expiry date, then Submit.

06

Directories & profiles — cross-tenant

From the Quick Way cards the admin opens global directories of students, University Super Admins, coordinators, and ambassadors. Each can be filtered by University, searched, and opened to a profile — with read oversight as the default, since day-to-day assignment of students stays a University Super Admin or coordinator operation.

  1. Tap a directory stat card — Total Students, University Super Admins, a coordinator card, or an ambassador card.
  2. Type in search (at least three characters for students).
  3. Tap Filter → narrow by University, Campus, Country, Program, and more → Apply.
  4. Tap a row to open the profile — student profile hub or read-only user profile, with university and campus clearly labelled.
  5. For outreach: enter selection mode on a list → select rows → Take Action → Send email.
Product

Global directories give the platform admin oversight without overreach: they can find and contact anyone, but operational actions like arrival assignment remain with the roles that own them.

QA focus

Search requires the minimum characters; the University filter scopes every directory; profiles show correct tenant/campus context; bulk email composes from a multi-select; assignment-style actions are absent (read oversight).

Marketing

"Find any student, admin, or ambassador on the platform — and reach a whole cohort with a single message."

07

Communities, events & reports

The admin oversees communities, events, and moderation across every tenant. They browse global catalogs, filter to one university, review pending communities and events where the wireframe grants it, and work reports through a clear status workflow to resolution.

  1. Communities: open from a community stat card or the Community tab; filter by university; tap any to view its profile, members, and feed.
  2. Pending communities: open a request, review name and description, and Approve or Reject with a reason.
  3. Events: open via the Events quick action, the Total Events stat, or the upcoming carousel; filter by university and tap for details.
  4. Pending events: on the cards, tap Approve or Reject (reason required to reject), or View All for the full queue.
  5. Reports: open Total Reports, filter by university, read a case, and move it through Assigned → Under Review → Escalated → Resolved; use My Assigned Reports for a personal queue.
Product

Oversight is cross-tenant but consistent: the same community, event, and report screens the single-university roles use, with a University filter layered on so the platform admin can work at scale or zoom into one tenant.

QA focus

The University filter scopes communities, events, and reports; approve/reject updates the relevant queue and requires a reason on reject; report status transitions persist; My vs All vs Assigned report views scope correctly.

Marketing

"Keep every campus vibrant and safe — review communities, approve events, and resolve issues across the whole platform."

08

Mail, configuration, support & account

Rounding out the console: emailing users from Mail, adjusting platform settings under Configurations, getting help from Support, and the top-bar essentials — language, notifications, and the profile menu with Log Out.

  1. Mail: open Mail from the sidebar to compose and send email to users; bulk outreach can start from a selection on a people list.
  2. Configurations: open Configurations to adjust platform and university settings; changes are staged and saved.
  3. Support: open Support for in-console help resources.
  4. Notifications & language: use the top-bar bell for alerts and the language switcher to change the console language.
  5. Account: open the top-bar profile menu (name + “Intoto Admin”) for account options and Log Out.
Product

Secondary destinations stay out of the way: Mail, Configurations, and Support live in the sidebar so the Dashboard keeps the focus, with language, notifications, and the account tucked into the top bar.

QA focus

Mail composes and sends; Configurations stage and save changes (Save appears when values differ); the language switch re-renders the console; the bell opens notifications; Log Out clears the session.

Marketing

"Everything an administrator needs in one console — message users, configure the platform, and get help without leaving Intoto."

Common questions

Intoto Admin FAQs

The questions admins, support, sales, and demo audiences ask most often about the platform role.

What can the Intoto Admin do?
Run the whole platform — onboard and manage universities, invite and control users across every tenant, and oversee communities, events, and reports. It's the top administrator role above any single university.
How is the Intoto Admin different from a University Super Admin?
Scope. The University Super Admin runs one university; the Intoto Admin operates across all universities, manages tenants themselves, and can switch university context. Only the Intoto Admin can add, suspend, or activate a university.
How do I onboard a new university?
Use the Add University quick action: enter the institution name, optional logo, identifier/domain, primary contact, and an initial campus, then optionally invite the first University Super Admin and submit. The tenant appears in the University List.
Can I focus on just one university?
Yes. Use the university context switcher in the header (or the University filter on any list) to scope counts and lists to one tenant, then select All Universities to return to the platform-wide view.
Which roles can I invite?
Any invitable role, including University Super Admin. A University is required for every university-scoped invite; a campus is required for operational roles but skipped for a Super Admin.
Do I assign students to coordinators?
Typically no — that's a University Super Admin or coordinator operation. The Intoto Admin has read oversight of student lists and profiles for volume and compliance, and reviews assignment status rather than running the assign wizard.
How do I handle moderation across tenants?
Open Total Reports, filter by university if needed, and move each case through Assigned → Under Review → Escalated → Resolved. My Assigned Reports gives you a personal queue.
What are the Quick Way cards, and what happens when I click one?
Quick Way cards are the stat tiles in the Dashboard Overview band — Students, Ambassadors, External Ambassadors, Third-Party Coordinators, Sub-coordinators, Event Coordinators, Community Coordinators, and University Super Admin. Each shows a live count, and clicking a card opens its own dedicated dashboard for that group, with its own list, stats, and actions.
What does my dashboard show?
A Dashboard Overview band of Quick Way stat cards plus analytics widgets — Student Admission, Student's Location, Communities, Event Status Distribution, Student Travel Plan, and Message — with a university filter, a date range, and per-widget export in the right rail.
Can one person be both an Intoto Admin and something else?
Yes. Multi-role accounts pick a role at Role Selection on login, or switch later from the role switcher; Home and tabs reload for whichever role is active.
What paid features can Intoto provide to a university?
Seven standalone modules: Ambassador Management, Travel Plan, Community, Chat, Event Management, User Management, and Mobility Program. A university can buy one, several, or all — see the Paid Features catalog for full detail on each.
Who provisions paid features for a university?
The Intoto Admin during university onboarding or when editing a tenant — setting Active state, contract dates, and User Management capacity. The University Super Admin sees the result read-only on My University → Features but cannot change subscriptions.
Can a university add features after go-live?
Yes. Intoto Admin enables additional modules when sales/onboarding requests it. Each new module gets its own Start/End dates and unlocks the related admin and student surfaces for that tenant only.
What happens if a module is not provisioned?
That functionality is hidden for the tenant — no related sidebar entries, dashboard cards, or student experiences. For example, without Chat provisioned, the Chats tab does not appear for that university's users.
How does User Management capacity work?
Intoto Admin sets Total Capacity and optional domestic/international limits when provisioning User Management. The university's Features tab shows live utilization; invites are blocked when capacity is exhausted until the limit is increased.
