At a glance

Who the External Ambassador is

An External Ambassador is an off-campus mentor — a recruitment partner, agency contact, alumnus abroad, or regional representative — invited with ambassador type External. In Figma (INTOTO-WebApp page EXTERNAL AMBASSADOR, node 1461:139005), they work from a web portal with a fixed sidebar, a global header, and five modules: Dashboard (student leads), Events, Community, Chat, and Support. Their public mentor profile appears on The Ambassador Platform — the student-facing Student Ambassadors directory — where prospects browse, filter, and start chats. Replies happen in the portal Chat inbox. They are not administrators: no inviting staff, assigning students, or approving content.

The one-paragraph version

An external ambassador signs into the web portal, monitors New Leads and response metrics on the Students dashboard, keeps their Profile current (badge External Ambassador, campus access, Ambassador Info), and answers prospects in Chat — including threads started on The Ambassador Platform, where they appear as a Staff-tagged card in the ambassador directory.

A day in the role

How the work flows

The external ambassador loop is web-first: monitor leads, stay visible on The Ambassador Platform, then be responsive in Chat — with an emphasis on the pre-arrival journey.

1

Sign in to the portal

Open the web app, complete Login or Signup, verify email if prompted, and choose External Ambassadors at role selection when the account holds multiple roles.

2

Check student leads

Land on the Students dashboard — scan New Leads, Total Leads, Total Responded, and Total Response Rate, then search or filter the leads table.

3

Keep the profile current

Open Profile — confirm the External Ambassador badge, Campus Access, and Ambassador Info so the public card on The Ambassador Platform stays trusted and complete.

4

Reply in Chat

Work the Chat sidebar — answer prospects who tapped Chat on the platform directory, and follow up on open lead threads.

5

Stay current on campus

Browse Events and Community so advice stays accurate; use Support when a question exceeds partner scope.

01

Getting in

External ambassadors are invited by university staff with ambassador type External. They sign into the INTOTO WebApp in the browser — not the mobile app's Auth0 flow — using email and password. Multi-role accounts land on a Choose your role to continue screen where External Ambassadors is one card alongside Ambassador, Student, and coordinator roles.

  1. Open the portal URL and tap Login or Signup.
  2. Enter email and password; optionally use Remember Me or Forget Password?
  3. If unverified, read the Verification Required! card and complete the inbox link before continuing.
  4. New users complete Create profile · Basic Information (photo, name, email, phone, country).
  5. When prompted, choose External Ambassadors on the role picker (Welcome! Choose your role to continue).
  6. Land on the Students dashboard with sidebar and header chrome visible.
Product

Web auth keeps off-campus partners out of the student mobile app while reusing the same lightweight signup pattern as The Ambassador Platform — one identity model, two products.

QA focus

Signup → verification → create profile → role picker → Students dashboard; External Ambassadors card routes to the correct shell; unverified login bounces to verification; role label in header reads External Ambassador after profile load.

Marketing

"Your partners log in from any browser — no app download, no campus SSO required."

02

Students & leads

Students is the external ambassador's command centre — not a mobile Home tab. Four KPI cards sit above a searchable, filterable leads table. This is where partners track outreach volume and response performance across prospects who engaged via The Ambassador Platform or other Intoto entry points.

  1. Land on Dashboard → Students after login (or tap Students in the sidebar).
  2. Read the four stat cards: New Leads, Total Leads, Total Responded, and Total Response Rate.
  3. Set the date range — Custom preset plus From Date and To Date pickers — then tap the export/download control to pull a report.
  4. Search with Search students…; toggle column grid, apply filters, or refresh the table.
  5. Scan rows for Students Name (avatar, name, email), Country, and Create Date.
  6. Tap a row to open the student profile overview and continue in Chat when needed.
Product

Lead KPIs speak the language of recruitment partners — volume, response, and rate — rather than campus governance stats. The table is the operational surface; chat is the action layer.

QA focus

KPI counts update after date filter changes; search debounces; pagination shows total count and page range; export produces data for the selected range; row tap opens the correct student profile.

Marketing

"See every prospect in one place — who's new, who's waiting, and how fast your team responds."

03

Profile

The web Profile page is what prospects read on The Ambassador Platform. The summary card shows photo, name, an External Ambassador badge, active status, campus count, and last login. Tabs cover Personal Information, FAQs, Settings, About App, and Delete Account. The Ambassador Info block — about text, programs, languages, favourite programs, clubs — is the public story students judge before they message.

  1. Open Profile from the header menu or the Edit Profile CTA on the summary card.
  2. On Personal Information, review Basic Detail (name, email, phone, passport, country) and Campus Access (assigned campuses such as Main, North, Downtown).
  3. Scroll to Ambassador Info — fill about text, ambassador type, country, program, academic year, campus, favourite programs, clubs, and qualifications.
  4. Tap Edit Profile to change photo or fields; save and confirm the badge still reads External Ambassador.
  5. Use FAQs, Settings, and About App for self-serve help; Delete Account requires confirmation.
Product

Campus Access makes multi-campus partnerships explicit — an external mentor may cover several locations while still appearing as one directory card. Profile completeness directly governs platform visibility and trust.

QA focus

Badge reads External Ambassador; campus list matches invite scope; Ambassador Info fields persist and sync to the platform card; Edit Profile updates the directory without stale photo or bio; Delete Account blocks re-login after confirmation.

Marketing

"Show prospects who you are and which campuses you cover — a complete profile is your invitation to connect before they ever apply."

04

Chats

Chat is where external ambassadors do their real work on the web. The sidebar opens a two-column inbox on desktop — thread list on the left, active conversation on the right — with support for individual chats, group chats, image and file attachments, audio, filters, and Start New Chat. Threads started on The Ambassador Platform arrive here when a prospect taps Chat on an ambassador card or profile.

  1. Open Chat from the sidebar.
  2. Scan the inbox for unread badges; use Chat filter or search to find a student by name.
  3. Tap a thread to read history and reply — text, images, PDFs, or audio per design.
  4. Use Start New Chat or Chat — Create Group flows when initiating outreach to selected students.
  5. Point students to Support, Events, or official channels when a question exceeds partner scope.
  6. Leave threads open for follow-up; counts update in the header bell and Chat list.
Product

One chat rail serves both platform-originated prospects and leads from the Students table — the external ambassador never needs to know which surface the student used first.

QA focus

Platform Chat CTA opens the correct thread in the portal inbox; send/receive works across attachment types; group create/name/add/select flows complete; empty inbox shows guidance; no bulk-message tools appear.

Marketing

"Real answers from a real partner — students message from the website, ambassadors reply from the portal."

05

The Ambassador Platform

The same Figma file pairs the external ambassador portal with The Ambassador Platform — the public Student Ambassadors directory (Powered by Intoto). Prospects sign up on the platform, browse All Ambassadors, filter by Staff (Figma frame Employee), open a card, read the full profile, and tap Chat. External ambassadors are the Staff-badged mentors in that directory; their portal profile feeds the card and profile page students see.

  1. A prospect opens the university's embedded platform URL and completes Signup → Verify → Create profile.
  2. They land on All Ambassadors, search, or tap filter chips — including Staff / Employee for external mentors.
  3. They tap an external ambassador's card to read photo, bio snippet, program, and country — or open the full Ambassadors Profile.
  4. They tap Chat or Ask me a question to start a conversation.
  5. The thread appears in the external ambassador's portal Chat inbox; the student name also surfaces on the Students leads table.
  6. Incomplete portal profiles lower trust and visibility on the platform directory — keep Ambassador Info current.
Product

Two products, one loop — the platform is top-of-funnel discovery; the portal is fulfilment. External ambassadors bridge recruitment partners to prospects without exposing admin tools on either surface.

QA focus

External ambassador with complete profile appears under Staff/Employee filter; card Chat opens a thread visible in portal Chat; profile fields on platform match portal Ambassador Info; cross-product message delivery is timely; see Intoto Ambassador Platform Feature & Flow Guide for visitor-side QA.

Marketing

"Featured on the university's public ambassador site — your partners become the face students meet before they download anything."

06

Events & community

External ambassadors participate in campus life from the browser — browsing and registering for events, exploring and joining communities, and posting as members. Figma includes full web flows for event lists, registration, community feeds, create-post, and share — without coordinator approve/reject controls.

  1. Open Events from the sidebar; browse upcoming items or My Events.
  2. Open an event detail and Register while the window is open.
  3. Open Community — explore, join, read feeds, and Create post (text, image, video, or document).
  4. Use share flows to forward content where the tenant allows.
  5. Escalate moderation issues via Support rather than acting as a coordinator.
Product

Events and community keep off-campus partners current on campus activity so platform chat advice stays accurate — participation without governance.

QA focus

Event register reflects on My Events; community join and member posting work; no approve/reject or assign-coordinator controls appear; share and report flows behave per design.

Marketing

"Stay close to campus life from anywhere — so every answer on the platform reflects what's actually happening."

07

Support & account

Rounding out the portal: Support for help requests, plus self-serve account actions from the header and profile tabs — language switch, notifications, Logout Confirmation?, and Delete Account.

  1. Open Support from the sidebar when a student question exceeds partner scope.
  2. Switch language from the header Eng (US) control; read notifications from the bell icon.
  3. From Profile → Settings or the header menu, tap Log Out and confirm.
  4. Use Delete Account on the profile tab for a destructive removal with explicit confirmation.
Product

Support and self-serve logout keep partners from becoming a university helpdesk bottleneck — the portal is narrow on purpose.

QA focus

Support form submits; language change re-renders chrome; logout clears session; delete blocks re-login; notification badge clears when threads are read.

Marketing

"Partners control their own session — help, logout, and account deletion are always one click away."

Common questions

External Ambassador FAQs

The questions external ambassadors, support, and demo audiences ask most often about the role.

How is an External Ambassador different from an Ambassador?
An Ambassador is usually a current student or alumnus — often on the mobile app and sometimes also holding the Student role. An External Ambassador is an off-campus partner invited with the External type; in Figma they work primarily from the web portal and appear on The Ambassador Platform with a Staff badge.
How is this different from The Ambassador Platform?
The Ambassador Platform is the public student directory — signup, browse, filter, chat. The External Ambassador portal is where partners manage leads, edit their profile, and reply. Students discover on the platform; external ambassadors fulfil in the portal. See Intoto Ambassador Platform Feature & Flow Guide for the visitor side.
Do external ambassadors use the mobile app?
Figma scopes External Ambassador to the INTOTO WebApp browser portal. Some tenants may also expose ambassador flows in the mobile app, but the web portal + platform pairing is the documented design for off-campus partners.
How do I get access?
University staff invite you with ambassador type External. You sign up or log in on the web portal, verify email, complete basic profile, choose External Ambassadors at role selection if prompted, then complete Ambassador Info so you go live on the platform directory.
What are New Leads and Total Response Rate?
New Leads counts recent prospect rows on the Students dashboard. Total Leads, Total Responded, and Total Response Rate summarise outreach volume and how often the partner replies — filterable by custom date range and exportable.
Where do students reach me?
Primarily through The Ambassador Platform — they filter by Staff, open your card, and tap Chat. The thread lands in your portal Chat inbox and the student appears on the Students leads table.
Why does my profile show a Staff badge on the platform?
Figma maps external and staff-type ambassadors to the Staff / Employee filter chip on All Ambassadors — distinguishing off-campus partners from student and alumni mentors. Your portal badge still reads External Ambassador.
Why is completing my profile so important?
Your Ambassador Info, photo, and campus access feed the public platform card and profile page. Incomplete profiles reduce trust and visibility in the directory; region and languages are especially strong match signals for international prospects.
Can I message lots of students at once?
No. Messaging is one-to-one or small group chat — there is no bulk-message surface in this role.
Can I approve events or communities?
No. You browse events and join and post in communities as a member; approvals belong to coordinator and admin roles.
What can I see on a student's profile?
The Student Profile - Overview shows the sections allowed by visibility rules for external partners — enough to support a conversation, with Chat as the primary action. No assign, suspend, or coordinator tools appear.
