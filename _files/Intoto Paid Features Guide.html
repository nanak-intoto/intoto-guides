At a glance

The Intoto product catalog for universities

Every university on Intoto subscribes to a custom mix of paid modules. A institution can start with a single feature — often User Management — and add Chat, Travel Plan, or others at any time. Each module is independent: its own contract dates, its own Active/inactive state, and its own set of admin tools and student experiences.

The one-paragraph version

Intoto Admin provisions modules when onboarding or updating a tenant. The University Super Admin sees what is live on My University → Features — read-only cards with Active status, Start/End dates, and (for User Management) seat capacity. When a module is Active, its sidebar entries and student-facing surfaces appear; when inactive, that functionality is hidden entirely.

Commercial model

How subscriptions work

Intoto does not require universities to buy the full platform. Sales and onboarding agree on which modules the tenant needs; Intoto Admin enables them on the backend. The university admin sees the result on the Features tab — they cannot toggle modules themselves.

  1. Sales / onboarding agrees module list, seat counts, and contract dates with the university.
  2. Intoto Admin provisions modules when creating the tenant or updating an existing university.
  3. University Super Admin opens My University → Features to confirm what is Active before inviting users or promising capabilities.
  4. When a new module is added mid-contract, it appears on the Features tab with its own Start/End dates; related sidebar tools unlock automatically.

03

Community

Portal card copy: Student connections & groups.

The Community paid feature lets people at a university discover, join, create, and engage with topic-based groups. It provides a social feed experience where members share posts, interact with content, and participate in discussions inside approved communities. Coordinators (and University Super Admins on web) create official communities and govern requests; students and Third-Party Coordinators propose new communities, join approved ones, and take part in discussions. When Active, sidebar Community tools, the mobile Community tab, feed/explore/my-community sections, posting, engagement, and report triage unlock. When inactive, those surfaces are hidden.

What Community unlocks

Feed for joined-community posts → Explore for discovery and joining → My Community for created/admin communities → request approval before a user-created community goes live → active community profiles with posts, members, rules, and engagement. Members can like, comment, reply, share, report, open profiles, and share posts or events into chat/community contexts.

Community creation workflow

The Community module separates trusted staff creation from user-submitted creation requests. A community is only discoverable and joinable after it becomes Active.

Coordinator / Super Admin creation

  1. Community Coordinator (or University Super Admin on web) creates a community on a topic.
  2. The community publishes instantly — no pending approval step.
  3. It becomes visible to relevant roles (Students, Third-Party Coordinators, and staff who can browse communities).

Student or Third-Party Coordinator request

  1. Student or Third-Party Coordinator submits a request to create a community.
  2. The request lands in the pending queue for Community Coordinator / University Super Admin.
  3. Until approval, the community remains in Pending state: only the creator and approving coordinator/admin can see it; other users cannot search, view, or join it.
  4. Approved — community is created and visible to relevant users; requester is notified.
  5. Rejected — community does not go live; requester receives a notification with the rejection reason.

Portal card copy: Create and manage campus events.

It supports the full lifecycle from event creation and approval to registration questions, QR-based attendance, community sharing, completion, ratings, and post-event feedback.

Common questions

Paid features FAQs

What paid features does Intoto offer universities?
Seven standalone modules: Ambassador Management, Travel Plan, Community, Chat, Event Management, User Management, and Mobility Program. Each can be purchased independently with its own contract dates and Active state.
Can a university buy only one or two features?
Yes. Intoto is à la carte — a university can subscribe to one module, several, or all seven, and add more at any time. There is no requirement to buy the full platform.
What is the Mobility Program feature?
Mobility Program is the academic exchange and international-office module. It gives Mobility Program Admins tools for application review, approval decisions, partner nominations, program and partner management, documents, reports, settings, and notifications. It also gives university leadership oversight of campus network, budget, programs, users, and institutional reports.
Who turns features on for a university?
Intoto Admin provisions modules during tenant onboarding or when sales requests a change. The University Super Admin sees the result on My University → Features but cannot toggle modules themselves.
Where does the university admin see subscribed features?
INTOTO WebApp → sidebar My University → Features tab. Each card shows the module name, description, Active badge, Start/End dates, and (for User Management) capacity and utilization figures.
What happens when a module is not Active?
Related admin sidebar entries, dashboard cards, and student experiences are hidden. For example, without Community active, students cannot create or join groups; without Chat, the Chats tab does not appear.
Can features be added mid-contract?
Yes. When Intoto provisions a new module, it appears on the Features tab with its own dates. Sidebar tools and student surfaces unlock automatically once Active.
What is User Management capacity?
Licensed seat pool for the tenant — Total Capacity, Utilization (percentage and count invited), and optional Domestic vs International splits. Invites are blocked when capacity is exhausted until Intoto Admin increases the limit.
Do students see which features are paid?
No. Students simply see the experiences their university has purchased — ambassadors, chat, communities, events, travel forms — without any subscription UI. Missing modules mean those areas of the app are not available.
Which module should a university buy first?
Most tenants start with User Management (required to invite anyone) and often add Chat early. Travel Plan and Ambassador Management are common before intake season; Community and Event Management follow as engagement programs grow. Mobility Program is the right starting point for institutions that want to run exchange applications, approvals, nominations, partner programs, and mobility reporting in Intoto. The mix is entirely up to the institution.
