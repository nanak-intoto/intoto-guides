At a glance

Who the Third Party Coordinator is

The Third Party Coordinator is an external partner — typically a travel, relocation, or arrival-services organisation — engaged by a university to receive incoming students and get them safely from arrival to campus. Inside Intoto they hold a pool of students, delegate batches to their own sub-coordinators with a service window and clear instructions, control exactly which student details each sub-coordinator can see, and watch every assignment move through its arrival states. They are not university administrators — they don't invite arbitrary users, approve events, or moderate communities.

The one-paragraph version

A coordinator works a pool of assigned students, runs the Assign Students flow (pick a sub-coordinator, set the date range, write the work brief, choose which fields are visible, then select students), then tracks each arrival through Assigned → Attended → Completed — handling reassign, remove, and unassign requests along the way, all scoped to their university.

A day in the role

How the work flows

The coordinator's loop runs the whole delegation lifecycle: receive a pool, place students, watch arrivals, and rebalance the load.

1

Sign in & scan the load

Land on an assignment-focused dashboard — Total Sub-Coordinators, students in the pool, and how many are assigned, attended, or completed.

2

Work the student pool

Open the list of students to place, search and filter, and select the batch heading to the same sub-coordinator.

3

Set up the assignment

Choose the sub-coordinator, set the from/to service window, write the work brief, and toggle which student fields they'll see — then confirm.

4

Track every arrival

Watch each student move through Assigned → Attended → Completed, and read the reason if a pickup didn't go to plan.

5

Rebalance the load

Reassign students to a different sub-coordinator, remove a placement, or action an unassign request when plans change.

01

Getting in

Sign-in is via the partner credentials issued for the university. Multi-role accounts choose Third Party Coordinator, and Home reloads with assignment Quick Way cards and the student pool — not student travel alerts or event queues.

  1. Open the app and sign in with email/password or SSO.
  2. Wait for the profile and role to load.
  3. Single role → land on the Third Party Coordinator dashboard; multi-role → choose the role at Role Selection.
  4. A pending invitation shows an alert at login — Accept or I'll do it later (envelope icon later).
  5. The four tabs appear — Home, Community, Chats, Profile.
Product

The role's home is purpose-built for delegation, so the coordinator opens directly into their pool and sub-coordinators rather than a generic admin surface.

QA focus

Single-role lands on the Third Party Coordinator dashboard; multi-role shows Role Selection; switching reloads assignment-centric sections (no student-travel alerts).

Marketing

"Open the app straight into your arrivals command centre — every student you're responsible for, in one place."

02

The dashboard

Home is an API-driven overview for delegation: a branded header, Quick Way tiles (Total Sub-Coordinators, students by status), quick actions, and lists for the pool and assignments — rendered in backend order.

  1. Land on Home; read the university header (tap for read-only context).
  2. Scan Quick Way — Total Sub-Coordinators opens your team; status tiles open the matching student lists.
  3. Open the student pool to see who still needs placing.
  4. Read the counts of Assigned, Attended, and Completed students to gauge progress.
  5. Pull to refresh after assigning or after a sub-coordinator marks status to update counts.
Product

Surfacing the pool and per-status counts up front means the coordinator always knows what still needs placing versus what's already moving.

QA focus

Assignment Quick Way cards render (not governance or event stats); Total Sub-Coordinators and status tiles open the correct filtered lists; pull-to-refresh updates counts after an assign or status change.

Marketing

"See your whole operation at a glance — who's waiting, who's been placed, and who's safely arrived."

03

The student pool

The pool is the list of students the university has handed to this partner but who aren't yet placed with a sub-coordinator. It's a searchable, paginated, filterable list — the starting point for every assignment.

  1. Open the students to assign list from Home or the relevant Quick Way tile.
  2. Search by name and apply filters (campus, date) to narrow the list.
  3. Tap a student row to open a read-only profile with the details your access allows.
  4. Use Select All or tap rows to build the batch heading to one sub-coordinator.
  5. Tap Assign to carry the selection into the assignment setup step.
Product

Selecting a batch before setup lets the coordinator place a planeload of students with one sub-coordinator, dates, and brief in a single pass — not one student at a time.

QA focus

List paginates and searches; filters re-query; Select All selects the loaded set; placed students leave the pool; read-only profiles open from rows and honour visibility.

Marketing

"Group arriving students by flight, day, or campus and place them together in seconds."

04

Assigning to sub-coordinators

The Assign Students flow is the heart of the role: a setup step captures the sub-coordinator, a from/to service window, a work brief, and field-level visibility — then the selected students are assigned in one request. Validation guards every field.

  1. From the pool with students selected, open Assign Students.
  2. Pick the Sub-Coordinator from your team (required).
  3. Set From Date and To Date for the service window (To must be on or after From).
  4. Write the Sub-Coordinator Work brief — what they need to do (required).
  5. Toggle which student fields the sub-coordinator may see (visibility access form), then confirm to assign the batch.
Product

Bundling who, when, what, and how-much-they-can-see into one setup makes delegation deliberate — the sub-coordinator gets exactly the brief and data they need, and nothing more.

QA focus

Missing sub-coordinator, missing dates, To-before-From, or empty work brief each block submit with the matching inline error; only toggled-on fields are sent as visible; a successful assign removes students from the pool and shows them as Assigned.

Marketing

"Delegate with confidence — set the dates, the brief, and exactly what each team member can see, in one screen."

05

Tracking arrivals & status

Once assigned, every student carries an arrival status the sub-coordinator updates from the field. The coordinator reads each Arrival Request Details record — status, who it's assigned to, dates, and the reason when something didn't go to plan.

  1. Open an assigned student to see Arrival Request Details.
  2. Read the Assignment Status — Assigned, Attended, or Completed.
  3. See the assigned sub-coordinator, who assigned it, and the assignment date range.
  4. When a status is Not Attended, read the reason (e.g. flight delayed, student not answering phone) and any notes.
  5. Pull to refresh to pick up status changes pushed by sub-coordinators.
Product

A single arrival record per student — status, owner, dates, and reason — gives the coordinator one trustworthy source of truth for every pickup, from assignment to completion.

QA focus

Status reflects the sub-coordinator's last update; Not Attended shows the chosen reason and notes; assigned-by, sub-coordinator, and date range render; refresh updates status without duplicating records.

Marketing

"Know where every student is in their arrival — and exactly why, the moment a plan changes."

06

Reassign, remove & unassign

Plans shift — a sub-coordinator falls ill, a flight moves, a student drops out. From the assigned-student list the coordinator can reassign a student to a different sub-coordinator, remove a placement, and respond to unassign requests raised from the field.

  1. Open the assigned students list and tap a student's more menu.
  2. Choose Assign Student, Remove Student, or Reassign Students.
  3. For Reassign, pick the new sub-coordinator and add an optional reason; confirm.
  4. Review unassign / reassign requests raised by sub-coordinators in Notifications and Accept or decline.
  5. Refresh — the student moves to the new owner or back to the pool as appropriate.
Product

Reassignment and unassign requests keep the operation resilient — the coordinator can rebalance load without losing the arrival history attached to each student.

QA focus

Reassign updates the owner and keeps the record; the reassignment reason is optional; remove returns the student to the pool; unassign requests appear in Notifications and Accept/decline updates state.

Marketing

"Adapt instantly — move a student to another team member the moment something changes, history intact."

07

Configurations & account

A Configurations screen sets the default visibility of student fields for sub-coordinators across assignments, and messaging keeps the team coordinated. The Profile tab holds account settings; community access is browse-only.

  1. Open Profile → Configurations to set which student fields sub-coordinators can see by default.
  2. Use the Chats tab to message sub-coordinators and students about arrivals and handovers.
  3. Tap the bell for notifications — new requests, status changes, arrivals due soon.
  4. Use the envelope for deferred role invitations.
  5. Open Profile for account info, FAQs, language, and logout; use Support to escalate.
Product

Centralising field visibility in Configurations means data-minimisation is the default — sub-coordinators only ever see what the partner has deliberately enabled.

QA focus

Configurations save and apply to new assignments; Configurations (not "Settings") is the label for this role; threads load and send; notifications deep-link to the right list; logout clears the session.

Marketing

"Protect student privacy by default — your team sees only what they need to do the job."

Common questions

Third Party Coordinator FAQs

The questions partners, support, and demo audiences ask most often about the role.

What is the Third Party Coordinator responsible for?
Receiving a pool of students from a university and getting them from arrival to campus by delegating batches to sub-coordinators with dates, a work brief, and controlled field visibility, then tracking each arrival.
Where do the students in my pool come from?
The university hands students to the partner organisation; they land in your pool and stay there until you assign them to a sub-coordinator.
How do I assign students to a sub-coordinator?
Select students, tap Assign, then choose the sub-coordinator, set the From/To dates, write the work brief, toggle the visible fields, and confirm — the batch is assigned in one request.
What gets validated on the assignment setup?
You must pick a sub-coordinator, set both dates with To on or after From, and enter a work brief; each missing or invalid field blocks submission until fixed.
How does field visibility work?
Only the fields you toggle on are shared with the sub-coordinator, and Configurations sets the default — data-minimisation by default.
What arrival statuses can a student have?
Assigned, Attended, and Completed — or Not Attended with a reason. The sub-coordinator sets these from the field; you read them on Arrival Request Details.
How do I reassign a student?
From the assigned list, use the student’s more menu and choose Reassign, pick the new sub-coordinator, add an optional reason, and confirm.
What’s the difference between removing and unassigning a student?
Remove pulls a student back to the pool; an unassign request is raised by a sub-coordinator and arrives in your Notifications for you to Accept or decline.
Can I create events or approve communities?
No. Your tools are focused on assigning students and coordinating their arrivals.
