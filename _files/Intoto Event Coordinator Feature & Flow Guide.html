At a glance

Who the Event Coordinator is

The Event Coordinator is university staff responsible for campus events end to end: creating events, reviewing submissions before they go live, monitoring registrations and attendance, and using analytics to understand programming by category. They operate within one university and are not the full administrator — they don't run invites for all roles, assign students to coordinators, or own the community approval queue.

The one-paragraph version

A coordinator creates and edits events, clears the pending event queue (approve or reject with a reason), tracks registrations and attendance from Event Details, and reads the events-by-category analytics to plan what to program next — all scoped to their university.

A day in the role

How the work flows

The coordinator's loop runs the whole event lifecycle: create, gate, run, and learn.

1

Sign in & scan the program

Land on an events-focused dashboard — Total Events, the category chart, and Upcoming / Pending / Completed carousels.

2

Create & submit events

Build events with schedule, venue, registration window, imagery, and category — saved or submitted for approval per tenant rules.

3

Clear the pending queue

Review submitted events for correct dates, venue, and policy, then approve to publish or reject with a reason.

4

Run event day

Track registrations, mark attendance, and review feedback from inside Event Details.

5

Plan with analytics

Read events-by-category and attendance trends to decide what to program next semester.

01

Getting in

Sign-in is via university credentials. Multi-role accounts choose Event Coordinator, and Home reloads with event Quick Way cards, carousels, and the analytics chart — not student travel alerts or community queues.

  1. Open the app and sign in with email/password or SSO.
  2. Wait for the profile and role to load.
  3. Single role → land on the Event Coordinator dashboard; multi-role → choose the role at Role Selection.
  4. A pending invitation shows an alert at login — Accept or Do it later (envelope icon later).
  5. The four tabs appear — Home, Community, Chats, Profile.
Product

The role's home is purpose-built for events, so the coordinator opens directly into their program rather than a generic admin surface.

QA focus

Single-role lands on the Event Coordinator dashboard; multi-role shows Role Selection; switching reloads event-centric sections (no student-travel alerts).

Marketing

"Open the app straight into your events command centre — everything you're running, in one place."

02

The dashboard

Home is an API-driven overview for events: a branded header, Quick Way (Total Events, Events Analytics), quick actions, the Upcoming / Pending / Completed carousels, and an events-by-category chart — rendered in backend order.

  1. Land on Home; read the university header (tap for read-only context).
  2. Scan Quick Way — Total Events opens the full catalog; Events Analytics opens reporting.
  3. Swipe the Upcoming, Pending, and Completed carousels; View All opens each list.
  4. Read the Events by Category chart (it may load just after the overview payload).
  5. Pull to refresh after creating or approving events to update counts.
Product

Separating events into Upcoming, Pending, and Completed mirrors the real lifecycle, so the coordinator always knows what needs action versus what's done.

QA focus

Event Quick Way cards render (not governance stats); carousels and View All work; the category chart shows a placeholder then loads; pull-to-refresh updates counts.

Marketing

"See your whole calendar at a glance — what's coming up, what needs approval, and what just wrapped."

03

Creating & editing events

A form-driven flow adds and updates events — title, description, schedule, registration window, location, category, cover image, and capacity. Depending on tenant rules, a new event lands in Pending or publishes to Upcoming.

  1. From the events list, tap Create / +.
  2. Fill required fields: title, description, date/time, registration start & end, location, category, cover image, capacity.
  3. Tap Save or Submit for approval — the event lands in Pending or Upcoming per auto-publish rules.
  4. To edit, open Event Details → Edit; some fields lock once registration opens or the event starts.
  5. Where provided, Cancel / Unpublish closes registration and moves the event to a cancelled/completed state.
Product

Locking certain fields after registration opens protects students who already signed up from surprise changes to dates or capacity.

QA focus

Required fields block save; an invalid date range (registration end before start) shows an inline error; post-registration field locks are enforced; cancel/unpublish closes registration.

Marketing

"Stand up a new event in minutes — schedule, venue, registration, and imagery in one clean form."

04

Approving events

The pending queue is the quality and compliance gate: events with wrong dates, missing venues, or policy issues shouldn't reach the student app. The coordinator reviews each submission and approves it to publish or rejects it with a reason.

  1. Open an event from Pending Events on Home or the pending list.
  2. Verify title, dates, registration start/end, location, image, category, and capacity.
  3. Approve (confirm if prompted) → the event leaves Pending and appears under Upcoming.
  4. Reject → enter a mandatory reason explaining what to fix; submit.
  5. Pull to refresh Home → Total Events updates and the queue reflects your decision.
Product

A single approval gate keeps the student-facing calendar trustworthy — every published event has been checked for correct dates, venue, and policy.

QA focus

Approve moves the event to Upcoming and updates counts; reject requires a non-empty reason; rejected events leave the queue or show rejected status; students never see pending events.

Marketing

"Every event students see has been reviewed — right dates, right place, on-policy."

05

Registrations & attendance

Event Details is the single-event hub. Beyond the overview, coordinators view who registered, mark who actually attended, and review post-event feedback — the proof of participation that feeds reports and follow-up.

  1. Open Event Details and go to the Registrations tab to see the registered student list.
  2. Search the list, open a student row for a read-only profile, or message per policy; export where provided.
  3. On or after event day, open Attendance and mark attended students (checkbox, scan, or manual list).
  4. Save — attendance is reflected in analytics.
  5. Review the Feedback tab for post-event ratings and comments.
Product

Keeping registrations, attendance, and feedback on the same event hub means the full record of an event lives in one place, from sign-up to outcome.

QA focus

Registration list loads and searches; attendance marking saves and flows into analytics; feedback summary renders; export works where provided; read-only student profiles open from rows.

Marketing

"Know who's coming, who showed up, and what they thought — every event, fully accounted for."

06

Event analytics

Beyond the Home category chart, a dedicated analytics screen shows KPIs and trends — total events, registrations, attendance rate, registrations over time, and events by category — with filters for date range, campus, and category.

  1. Tap Events Analytics on Home (Quick Way).
  2. Apply filters — date range, campus, category.
  3. Read the KPI tiles and the bar/pie charts.
  4. Identify under-used categories to plan future programming.
  5. Drill through to a filtered event list where the frame supports tap-through.
Product

Analytics close the loop from "run events" to "program better" — the coordinator plans next semester from real attendance and category data, not guesswork.

QA focus

Filters re-query and update charts; KPI labels are API-driven; charts show placeholders while loading; tap-through (where present) opens a correctly filtered list.

Marketing

"Turn every event into insight — see what's working and program more of what students show up for."

07

Chat & account

Messaging supports the event work — registration questions, venue changes, accessibility requests — and notifications surface new submissions and reminders. The Profile tab holds account settings; community access is browse-only.

  1. Open the Chats tab to message students and staff about events; search for a user where compose is available.
  2. Tap the bell for notifications — new pending submissions, registration thresholds, events starting soon.
  3. Use the envelope for deferred role invitations.
  4. Browse the Community tab to promote events in posts (no community approval powers here).
  5. Open Profile for account info, FAQs, language, and logout; use Support to escalate.
Product

Notifications are tuned to event timing — submissions, registration thresholds, and start reminders — so the coordinator acts at the right moment.

QA focus

Threads load and send; notifications deep-link to Event Details or the pending list; no community approval controls appear; logout clears the session.

Marketing

"Stay on top of every event — reminders when it matters and a direct line to attendees."

Common questions

Event Coordinator FAQs

The questions coordinators, support, and demo audiences ask most often about the role.

What is the Event Coordinator responsible for?
Campus events end to end within one university — creating events, reviewing submissions, tracking registrations and attendance, and reading analytics. They don’t manage users or own community approvals.
How do I create a new event?
From the events list tap Create / +, fill the required fields — title, description, date/time, registration window, location, category, cover image, and capacity — and Save or Submit for approval.
How do I approve or reject a submitted event?
Open it from Pending Events, verify the details, then Approve to publish or Reject. Rejection requires a mandatory reason explaining what to fix.
Why can’t I edit some fields after an event is live?
Certain fields lock once registration opens or the event starts, to protect students who already registered from surprise changes to dates or capacity.
What’s the difference between Upcoming, Pending, and Completed?
Upcoming are published future events students can register for; Pending await your approve/reject decision; Completed have passed. Each has its own carousel and list.
How do I see who registered for an event?
Open Event Details → Registrations to see the list, search it, open read-only student profiles, and export where provided.
How do I mark attendance?
On or after event day, open Attendance in Event Details, mark attended students, and save. Attendance feeds into analytics.
What does the analytics screen show me?
KPI tiles and charts — total events, registrations, attendance rate, registrations over time, and events by category — with filters for date range, campus, and category.
Can I approve communities or invite users?
No. Community approval belongs to the Community Coordinator, and inviting or suspending users to the University Super Admin.
