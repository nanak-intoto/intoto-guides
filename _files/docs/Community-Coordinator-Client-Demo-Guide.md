# Intoto — Community Coordinator
## Client Demo Guide

**Design reference:** [Intoto Wireframe — Community Coordinator (node 5088-26466)](https://www.figma.com/design/BSt8IrK987At3ZBXfyUbyY/Intoto-wireframe?node-id=5088-26466&page-id=5051%3A661)

**Audience:** Client demos, stakeholders, pre-sales  
**Demo length:** 12–16 minutes  
**Version:** 1.0

---

## 1. Demo objective

Demonstrate that the **Community Coordinator** is the university’s **gatekeeper and publisher** for student groups:

- one **Home** dashboard for pending volume, live communities, and member scale
- a clear **approve / reject** workflow with reasons students can act on
- **instant official communities** created by staff (no student wait state)
- safe, in-app **Community** and **Chats** support — without exposing full admin tooling

---

## 2. Pre-demo checklist

| Item | Why it matters |
|------|----------------|
| Community Coordinator login | Primary persona |
| ≥ 1 **pending** community request | Core approve/reject story |
| ≥ 1 **active** community | Browse feed / profile |
| Optional: second **Student** device | Show submit → approve loop |
| Pending count > 0 on Quick Way | Visual proof of queue |
| Figma prototype link open | Align stakeholders on UX |

## 3. Talk track (30 seconds)

> "Students propose communities; the Community Coordinator decides what goes live.  
> Staff can also launch official groups instantly. Everything stays moderated and on-brand inside Intoto — not scattered across unofficial chat apps."

---

## 4. Step-by-step demo flow

### Step 1 — Login and context (1 min)

**Do**
1. Log in as **Community Coordinator**.
2. Point to university **logo + name** on Home.
3. Mention role label or **role switcher** if multi-role.

**Say**
- "This role owns community safety and publishing — not events, not assigning arrival coordinators, not inviting the whole staff roster."

---

### Step 2 — Home dashboard (3 min)

**Do**
1. Scroll Home top to bottom.
2. Highlight **Quick Way**: e.g. **Total Communities**, **Pending Communities**, **Community Members** (use labels from your build).
3. Highlight **Quick Actions**: **Communities**, **Create Community**, **Support**.
4. Swipe **Pending** and **Active** carousels if shown.
5. Point to any **community analytics** chart on Home.

**Say**
- "Pending is the inbox — students are waiting for a decision."
- "Active is what’s already in student discovery."
- "Member counts help you see where engagement is concentrating."

**Optional:** Pull to refresh.

---

### Step 3 — Approve or reject a request (3 min)

**Do**
1. Tap a **Pending Communities** card → open details.
2. Walk **name**, **description**, **images**.
3. **Approve** one request **or** **Reject** with a clear sample reason.

**Say**
- "Rejection always carries a reason so creators can fix and resubmit — not a silent dead end."
- "Approval makes the group visible in the student app immediately."

**Fallback:** Use Student device to submit a test community before this step.

---

### Step 4 — Create an official community (2 min)

**Do**
1. **Create Community** quick action.
2. Fill name, description, images (minimal required fields).
3. Submit → show **success** and live community.

**Say**
- "University-run groups do not sit in the student approval queue — they publish straight away."

---

### Step 5 — Community tab and feed (2 min)

**Do**
1. Open **Community** tab.
2. Open the community you approved or created.
3. Scroll feed; optionally show **Create post** welcome message.

**Say**
- "Coordinators can participate and moderate where the wireframe provides tools."

---

### Step 6 — Student loop (optional, 2 min)

**Do**
1. Student device: **Create Community** → submission confirmation.
2. Coordinator device: approve.
3. Student device: community appears in **Discover**.

**Say**
- "Closed loop — request, review, live — without leaving Intoto."

---

### Step 7 — Chats and close (1 min)

**Do**
1. Mention **Chats** for creator questions after rejection.
2. **Support** for policy escalations.

## 6. Client FAQ

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| vs University Super Admin? | CC is community-focused; USA has full user and cross-domain stats. |
| vs Event Coordinator? | Events vs communities — separate roles and dashboards. |
| Can students post before approval? | No — pending groups are not public until approved. |
| Customize dashboard cards? | Yes — server-driven overview per role. |

---

## 7. Do not demo on Community Coordinator role

- Manage Users / Add User / Assign Students
- Event pending approval and Events Analytics as primary story
- Full USA Quick Way grid

Switch roles explicitly on multi-role accounts.
