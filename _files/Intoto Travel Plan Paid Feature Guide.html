Feature overview

Arrival operations without spreadsheet chasing.

Travel Plan gives universities a clean view of student arrival details, travel timelines, and campus arrival status. Staff can see who has submitted details, who is in transit, and who needs follow-up.

When Active, the module unlocks Travel Plan dashboard cards, sidebar navigation, campus travel lists, status filters, student travel records, and arrival monitoring. When inactive, travel screens and campus travel tabs are hidden.

FAQs

Travel Plan questions clients usually ask.

What is Travel Plan?
Travel Plan is the paid module for collecting and tracking student arrival information. It helps university teams know who has submitted travel details, who is still missing information, who is in transit, and who has reached campus.
What information does the student provide?
Typical travel details include arrival date/time, destination campus, airport or station, flight or train number, transport mode, accommodation or check-in notes, emergency contact, and remarks where configured.
Can staff see students who have not submitted plans?
Yes. The value of the module is visibility into both submitted plans and missing plans, so teams can follow up early instead of discovering gaps during arrival week.
Can staff see incomplete travel plans?
Yes. Incomplete or unclear plans can be surfaced for follow-up. Coordinators can check missing dates, unclear destinations, late arrivals, or missing emergency contact details.
How does Travel Plan help during airport arrival?
Arrival teams can use expected arrival windows, transport mode, and destination campus to plan pickup, check-in support, housing handoff, and emergency response.
Can records be filtered by campus?
Yes. The campus travel view helps local teams see only the students arriving to their campus or destination area, which is important for multi-campus institutions.
What statuses can the team track?
The workflow can represent states such as missing, submitted, incomplete, in transit, delayed, arrived, or follow-up needed. The exact labels depend on the product configuration.
Does Travel Plan replace emergency communication?
No. It gives teams better preparation and visibility, but emergency communication should still follow the university's official safety and escalation policy.
Can Travel Plan help orientation planning?
Yes. Knowing arrival batches helps staff plan orientation sessions, check-in counters, welcome support, and late-arrival follow-up.
Can staff mark a student as arrived?
Yes, the operating flow supports closing the loop when a student reaches campus or completes the arrival checkpoint. This changes the record from expected or in transit to arrived.
Can students update travel details after submitting?
The flow should allow students to correct travel details if the university enables updates. This is useful for delays, flight changes, accommodation changes, or wrong information entered earlier.
Who reviews travel plans?
University staff, arrival coordinators, or the University Super Admin team review travel plans depending on the tenant's operating model. The goal is to find gaps and plan arrival support.
How does Travel Plan help leadership?
It gives a readiness signal before intake: how many students have submitted plans, how many are missing information, and how many have arrived. That helps leadership understand operational risk.
Can Travel Plan be purchased without Community or Events?
Yes. Travel Plan is a standalone paid module. A university can purchase it independently if its immediate need is arrival tracking and intake operations.
What happens when inactive?
Travel Plan cards, sidebar entries, travel lists, campus travel tabs, student travel forms, status tracking, and arrival-status workflows are hidden for that university.
