Feature overview

A university-owned social layer for campus groups.

Community gives the university a governed place for students, staff, and third-party partners to discover groups, join discussions, share useful content, and build belonging without moving conversations to unmanaged public channels.

What the client gets

When Community is Active, the app unlocks Feed, Explore, My Community, community profiles, community creation, pending request review, post creation, likes, comments, replies, sharing, report handling, and notifications. When inactive, those surfaces are hidden from students and staff.

What users can do

Every visible action in the Community feature.

Discover

Explore approved communities

Users browse available university communities, open community profiles, read descriptions and rules, view member counts, and join relevant groups.

Create

Create or request a community

Coordinators and Super Admins can publish communities instantly. Students and Third-Party Coordinators submit requests that wait for approval.

Post

Publish content in joined groups

Members can create text posts, image posts, video posts, document posts, and event-style share cards inside active communities.

Engage

Like, comment, and reply

Users like posts, open like lists, comment, like comments, reply to comments, like replies, and continue discussions inside the community thread.

Share

Share posts and community content

Content can be shared to eligible community or chat contexts where sharing is available, helping useful posts travel without losing source context.

Protect

Report and moderate content

Users can report posts from the three-dot menu. Coordinators review reports, remove inappropriate content, and keep community standards clear.

FAQs

Community questions clients usually ask.

Can students create their own communities?
Yes. Students can submit a community creation request with the community name, topic, description, audience, and purpose. The community is not visible to others until a Community Coordinator or University Super Admin approves it.
Can staff create official groups instantly?
Yes. Community Coordinators and University Super Admins can create official communities directly because those roles are trusted governance owners. These communities can go live without waiting in the student-request queue.
Can Third-Party Coordinators request communities?
Yes. Third-Party Coordinators can request a community where the university allows that role to participate. Their request follows the same approval path as a student request.
What happens before a requested community is approved?
The community stays in a pending state and is not discoverable by other users. The requester and approving staff can see the request, while normal users cannot join or post in it.
What can users do after joining a community?
Joined users can see that community's posts in their Feed, open the community profile, publish allowed post types, like posts, comment, reply, share, and report inappropriate content.
What types of posts are supported?
Community supports text posts plus media and attachment-style posts such as single images, multiple images, videos, documents, and shareable event cards where Event Management sharing is enabled.
Can users comment and reply?
Yes. Users can comment on posts, reply to comments, like comments, like replies, and keep discussions attached to the original post instead of creating disconnected conversations.
Can users share a community post to Chat?
Yes, when Chat is Active and sharing is allowed. Sharing a community post into Chat keeps the post context available for private or group follow-up.
Can events be shared inside communities?
Yes, where Event Management and Community are both Active and the app enables event sharing. The event can appear as a shareable event card inside the selected community.
Can users see who liked a post?
The Community experience can expose like-count details so users can understand engagement around a post. Where user lists are available, tapping the like count can open profiles subject to privacy and role permissions.
How is unsafe content reported?
Users report inappropriate content from the post menu. The report carries the post context and reason to the coordinator/admin review process.
Who moderates community content?
Community Coordinators and University Super Admins own moderation. They can review reports, remove inappropriate content, reject requests, archive communities, and follow up where needed.
Can a university have multiple Community Coordinators?
Yes. A university can staff the Community module as a team. Multiple coordinators can share responsibility for creation, approvals, reports, and moderation.
How does Community help students?
Students get a university-owned place to find peers, ask questions, join interest groups, share experiences, and receive information through communities they actually care about.
What happens when the Community module is inactive?
Community navigation, group discovery, create/join flows, feeds, posting, comments, replies, sharing, reporting, and moderation surfaces are hidden for that university.
